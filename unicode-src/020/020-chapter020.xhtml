Vainu Bappu in a nutshell

1927 - Born on August 10, 1927.
1943 - Graduated from Nizamia College, Hyderabad.
1946 - First scientific paper appeared in Current Science.
1949 - Joins Harvard University on a Govt. of Hyderabad scholarship. W hile at Harvard discovered Bappu-BokNewkirk Comet and was awarded the Donohoe Medal by the ASP.
1952 - Awarded Carnegie Fellowship for 1951-52. Spent one year at Mt. W ilson and Palomar Observatories. Bappu was awarded Ph.D. Returns to India after visiting many observatories in USA and Europe.
1954 - Appointed as Chief Astronomer at the UPSO, Varanasi.
1955 - UPSO observatory shifted from Varanasi to Manora Peak.
1956 - Weds Yemuna.
1957 - Published his seminal paper on W ilson-Bappu Effect in Astrophysical Journal in 1957.
1960 - Appointed as Director, KodaikanalObservatory. Modernizes observational facilities at Kodaikanal.
1962 - Starts site survey for a stellar Observatory at Kavalur.
1967 - Commissioned 38 cm telescope at Kavalur.
1970 - Leads a team to observe total solar eclipse at Mexico.
1971 - Birth of the Indian Institute of Astrophysics.
1972 - One-meter Zeiss telescope commissioned at Kavalur. Founded the Astronomical Society of India and was elected the President.
1973 - Puts up a proposal for an for Kavalur.
1975 - IIA headquarters shifted from Kodaikanal to Bangalore.
1977 - Co-authored a paper on the Uranus rings using the 1-meter telescope at Kavalur, which was published in Nature. Was presented the Sri Hari Om Ashram Award in Physics.
1979 - Elected President of the International Astronomical Union.
1980 - Leads an expedition to observe the solar eclipse at Jwalagere.
1981 - Was honored with the National Award of “Padma Bhushan”.
1982 - Passed away on August 19, 1982.
1983 - Was posthumously awarded the S.N. Bose Medal of the INSA for 1980.
