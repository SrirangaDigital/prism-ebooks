Create a Problem

One of my senior colleagues always narrates an incident, whenever he takes a session on Productivity Improvement Program (PIP). Once it appears, a team of Japanese consultants visited our company. They asked my colleague, Was there any problem? As things were going smooth and our company was doing well, he obviously replied that he had no problem. “Then, why don’t you create a problem?” was the simple suggestion from the Japanese.

Later, he understood what the Japanese meant by creating a problem. A re-look over the present way of working or target setting would give new insight to What more can be done. This insight would enable one to set a new benchmark. Once it is done, the objective of creating a problem is achieved! The logic and the need for creating a problem appeared so simple.

To a Kaizen-mind, excellence has no lid. One can always excel the excellence. Turning a Nelson’s eye to the problems is a well nigh natural behaviour. “The problem is not that we don’t have solutions – the problem is that we don’t want to see a problem!” was the genuine worry of Peter Drucker. If this is the state of affairs, the strategic move to create a new problem is much above the common understanding.

Creation of a problem is the key to success, I realized. PQCD – Productivity, Quality, Cost (effectiveness) and Delivery – can be taken care of by just creating a problem. The kernel, which I could get out of my colleague’s experience, was something, which would sound wrong English. Not the grammar, but the underlying meaning that is important to allure wisdom:

Better the best by creating a problem

A single lie destroys a whole reputation of integrity

– Baltasar Gracion
