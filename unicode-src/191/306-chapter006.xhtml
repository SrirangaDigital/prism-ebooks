MIGRATION OF ANIMALS

Earlier, we saw that the orangutans and the gibbons are now found, only in Asia, i.e. Indonesia, Malaysia etc., and not in Africa. Similarly, gorillas and chimps live only in Africa and not in Asia. As they are all of the same lineage there should have been a common place from where they all evolved. To get confirmation for this idea, I investigated the presence of old world monkeys and the Loris family. They are now found in three isolated pockets in Africa, India and South East Asia. Fig. 3.3 gives the natural range of old world monkeys and Fig. 3.4 gives the natural range of the Loris super family. A look at these figures clearly indicates that these two groups must have lived on the Central land mass in the Indian Ocean which had land connections to Africa, India and S.E. Asia. When the land connection vanished due to the rise in the sea level, the animals would have got isolated in those three pockets. This fact indirectly confirms that Kumari Continent existed sometime in the past. In order to get more support for the idea, I also studied the migration of cats and dogs and they also show a strong proof that they too could have had a similar origin.

Till recently, it was believed that wolves were of North American origin. Genetic studies, however, show that the wolves were in India at least one million years ago. This revelation is due to the work done by Yandvendradev Jhala of Wildlife Institute of India (Dehradun). Ramesh Agarwal and Lalji Singh of the Centre for Cellular and Molecular Biology (Hyderabad). Their conclusion is that wolves evolved near India and not in North America. The Hyderabad group compared twenty one wolves in the Darjeeling Zoo. R.C. Fleisher of Smithsonian Institute compared the genetic clocks of 700 wolves from India, Middle East and North America. Both groups came to similar conclusions that all this happened a million year ago when the Himalaya was young. As it has been proved that wolves had migrated to different parts of the world from India, India or near by landmass must have been the cradle of wolf evolution.

There is also some useful information about the migration of cats based on the research conducted by Warren E. Johnson and Stephen J. O Brien of the National Cancer Institute and colleagues elsewhere. According to them, sea levels were low from one million to six million years ago enabling the first modern cats to spread from Asia to the West and Africa creating the caracal lineage and generating the ocelot, lynx and puma lineages. This again reinforces the theory that life originated in the landmass in the Indian Ocean near India and spread all over the world.
