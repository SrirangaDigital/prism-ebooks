Preface

The Hata Yoga Pradipika says that, “One becomes a sidda (perfect) through practice alone and not by merely reading the scriptures or changing the colour of the cloth or discussing about it. Anyone young, old, weak or sick who practices relentlessly with dedication will attain perfection in yoga”.

The first section in this book deals with Surya Namaskara. Surya Namaskara also referred to as Sun Salutation is gaining popularity all over the world. Many, substitute intense workouts with Surya Namaskaras and label these as Suryathons. The scriptures propound that whatever one does should be divinised and offered as Yajna (worship) in order to derive full benefits. With this understanding a Suryathon can be transformed into a Surya Yajna.

Surya Namaskara is undoubtedly a complete solution for one’s psychosomatic -- body and mind well-being. It de-stresses the mind and strengthens the circulatory, respiratory, digestive, excretory, reproductive, nervous and endocrine systems.

In an exercise to analyse the benefits and effects of Surya Namaskara on my body, I performed 108 repetitions of the 16-step Surya Namaskara in 18 minutes and recorded the following observations:

  • Rise in heart rate from 80 bpm to 160 bpm.
  • Drop in blood pressure from 120 /80 to 100 / 60
  • Decrease in body weight by about half a kilogram.

The effects of Surya Namaskara reach far beyond the psychosomatic level to benefit the practitioner at the soul (jiva) level.

Shat Prashnopanishad says that the soul (jiva) is bound by 15 things and they are:

1. Kham: Space 2. Vayu: Air 3. Agni: Fire 4. Aapa: Water 5. Pritvi: Earth 6. Karmendriya and Jnanendriya: Legs, hands, mouth, anus, genital, eyes, ears, nose, tongue, skin 7. Mana: Mind 8. Ahara: Food -- type of food we eat 9. Veerya: Stamina -- getting fat or lean 10. Tapa: Thinking 11. Mantra: Talking 12. Karma: Action- what we think; that we do. 13. Loka: Our possessions, properties 14. Nama: Name and Fame- we do things for name and fame 15. Shradda: Belief -- family traditions, social beliefs, our convictions etc.

The number 16 represents the jiva (soul), number 17 represents Prakruti (Lakshmi) and number 18 represents the Purusha (Narayana).

By divinising Surya Namaskara the soul (jiva) can be progressed towards Purusha. This is the definition of Yoga -- joining the individual soul (jiva) with the Supreme (Purusha, Paramatma).

The second section of this book explains Pranayama in detail. Pranayama helps in purifying the nadis (subtle pathways of prana / breath). When the nadis are purified - the frequency of breathing can be reduced thereby leading to longevity, digestive fire becomes activated, inner sound can be heard and perfect health can be enjoyed. Pranayama is the link between the body and the mind. Pranayama helps in improving the body immunity. The Sandhya Vandana pranayama has been included in the Pranayama section for the benefit of all serious spiritual seekers.

Mudras and Bandhas are dealt with in the third section. Mudras and Bandhas help in conserving and redirecting the energy within the body. The inner energy must be channelised properly for the inherent personality to blossom.

The fourth section deals with acupressure points in the palms and feet. Diseases frequent the body when the flow of energy in the nadis is obstructed. When these blocks are cleared, the body becomes disease free. The energy blocks can be removed and the whole body can be energised in a fun and simple way by combining star jumps with hand-claps.

For the benefit of practitioners, we have embedded QR codes linking the techniques discussed in the book to related Vasudeva Kriya Yoga videos.
