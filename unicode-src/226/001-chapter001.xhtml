Surya Namaskara

Everyone has immense energy within which only a few are able to tap into, while in the larger majority it remains dormant as coiled up energy or potential energy. Surya Namaskara is one of techniques for awakening the coiled up energy or kundalini Shakti. Surya Namaskara helps in converting the potential energy to kinetic energy.

The scriptures says that sharira madyam khalu dharma sadhanam (शरीरमाद्यं खलु धर्मसाधनम्) -- through the healthy body alone one can do sadhana. Here the health refers to health of both the body and the mind. The same thing is told in Upanishad as Mana eva manushyanam karanam bandha mokshayoho -- (मन एव मनुष्याणां कारणं बन्धमोक्षयोः), “as the mind, so the man; bondage or liberation are in your own mind.” Having a healthy body without a healthy mind is of no use. It is true the other way also, having a healthy mind and a weak body is also of no use. Doing Surya Namaskara regularly helps in converting slothful body and drowsy mind to dynamic body and alert mind. Surya Namaskara is the source of health for the body, mind and the soul. This is mentioned in the scripture as

Arogyam bhaskara:d ichche:th
Sriyam ichche:th utha:sana:th
sankara:th jna:na manvichcheth
Mo:ksham ichcheth jana:rdana:th

आरोग्यं भास्करादिच्छेत् श्रियमिच्छेद् हुताशनात् ।
शंकराद् ज्ञानमन्विच्छेत् मोक्षमिच्छेद् जनार्दनात् ।।

“If you want just health, you have to worship the Lord in the form of Bhaskara (Sun). And if you want just wealth, you need to worship the Lord through the fire. But if you want just knowledge, you have to worship Lord Shiva. But if you want a release from all bondages, you need to worship the Lord in the form of Vishnu.”

Bhaskara means the radiant one. If you think sun is the radiant one you will get only good health. But if you realise the Lord is the most resplendent one who has given the light even to the sun, then that same Surya Namaskara becomes a path of moksha (liberation).

Significance of Surya Namaskara (Sun Salutation)

The benefits of Surya Namaskara are mentioned in scriptures like Valmiki Ramayan, Mahabharata and other scriptures. Surya Namaskara is a process of invoking the Grace of the Lord through sun. Sun is the most brilliant gross object we all can see. When we see this brilliant sun, we should be reminded of most resplendent Lord who gave this brilliance to the sun. Surya Namaskara signifies the “Path of Light”. The Brihadaranyaka Upanishad summarises the significance of Surya Namaskara as-

Om Asato Maa Sad-Gamaya
Tamaso Maa Jyotir-Gamaya
Mrtyor-Maa Amrtam Gamaya-

असतो मा सद् गमय । तमसो मा ज्योतिर्गमय । मृत्योर्मा अमृतं गमय ।।

Lead us from imperfection to Perfection, from darkness to Light, from death to Immortality.

Like Gayatri mantra, Surya Namaskara is also a process of invoking the Lord in the sun. Surya Namaskara is for the body, mind and soul. Every morning at sunrise, doing Surya Namaskara facing the East is believed to bestow - good health, long life, increased energy level (Prana Shakti) or stamina, glow to the face, improved eye sight and a personality that is radiant and effulgent like the sun.

It is also believed that there will not be any dearth in the practitioner's life.

With the regular practice of Surya Namaskara, oxygen content in the blood increases, body immunity improves and the respiratory, digestive, circulatory, nervous, reproductive and excretory systems get strengthened. Surya Namaskara is beneficial for people with blood pressure, diabetes, obesity and depression problems. Above all, it helps in invoking the Grace of the Lord in the Sun who is also in our heart. The same thing is mentioned in Taittiriya Upanishad as Sa yaschayam purushe yaschasavaditye sa ekah - the Lord in this person and Sun God is the same.

The sun enlivens and sustains the whole world. At dawn birds start singing with joy seeing the sun rise. As the sun sets, all birds go to their nests. We also feel energetic as the sun rises and we feel drained of energy as the sun sets. There is a link between the sun and the energy level in the body. By doing Surya Namaskara, we are linking the prana shakti (energy level) in our body to the prana shakti in the sun. This linking process is Surya Namaskara. There is a prana in the sun, and within the prana the Supreme Lord resides. In our body also, there is a Prana and within this Prana the Supreme Lord resides. The Rig Veda mantra describes the benefits of praying to the Lord in the sun.

In summary, the Vedic mantras say: ‘Give us a healthy body with long life so that we can continue our sadhana (practice) for the whole life. Let there be no deformity, accidents or untimely death for us which curtails our sadhana. May we get a permanent body, i.e. liberation, so that there is no pain of repeated birth and death.’

There is a numerology in Surya Namaskar. To understand the scriptures we need know the numerology. Numerology gives a clear direction to philosophy. Surya Namaskara given here has 16 steps. This has numerological significance. Bhagawat Gita has 18 chapters. The number 18 is divine - the highest. The number 18 means the Lord (Purushottama). The number 17 means akshara (Lakshmi). The number 16 is the individual soul. This individual soul is bound and caught up by 15 things. By His Grace if this 16 (individual soul) turns its attention to 18 (the Lord), 17 (akshara -Lakshmi) paves the way to the souls journey towards Him. If not, 17 (akshara -Lakshmi) binds 16 (individual soul) with 15 aspects. This is the message of the 15th Chapter Bhagawat Gita and this is the message of whole Bhagawat Gita. The 15 things which binds the individual soul are:

  1. Kham: Space
  2. Vayu: Air
  3. Agni: Fire
  4. Aapa:Water
  5. Pritvi: Earth
  6. Karmendriya and Jnanedriya: Legs, hands, mouth, anus, genital, eyes, ears, nose, tongue, skin
  7. Mana: Mind
  8. Ahara: Food -- type of food we eat
  9. Veerya: Stamina -- getting fat or lean
  10. Tapa: Thinking
  11. Mantra: Talking
  12. Karma: Action- what we think, that we do.
  13. Loka: Our possessions, properties
  14. Nama: Name and Fame- we do things for name and fame
  15. Shradda: Belief -- family traditions, social beliefs, our convictions etc.

The 16 step Surya Namaskar is the soul's prayer- may this soul (16) turn its attention to the Lord (18) and may Lakshmi (17) remove all the hurdles in our path.

The number zero in 108 also represents a complete number. Number 108 represents the Supreme Lord's number. When we do 108 Surya Namaskar with 16 steps in each round we will be doing a total of 108*16 = 1728 steps. If we add this 1+7+2+8 this again become 18 which is a divine number. Through Surya Namaskar we are invoking the Supreme Lord. Yoga means union and Surya Namaskar is a process of linking the individual soul (16) to the Supreme Lord (18).

Surya Namaskara technique:

For the success in Surya Namaskara the following disciplines are recommended:

  1. The best time to do the Surya Namaskara is before the sun rise in empty stomach.
  2. Before Surya Namaskara empty your bowel, brush your teeth and have a bath.
  3. Wear comfortable clothing and with cheerful mind do Surya Namaskara.
  4. Perform Surya Namaskara in a well-lit, aerated space, on a flat ground facing east in the morning and west in the evening.
  5. If you sweat, rub the sweat on the body.
  6. While doing the Surya Namaskara breathe only through your nose.
  7. Regularity in practice is the key to success.
  8. Try to do Surya Namaskara daily in the same place as an offering to Surya Narayana (Lord).
  9. Do Yoga Nidra or Shavasana after Surya Namaskara.
  10. Make a habit of going to bed early at night and rising up before sun rise.
  11. Ahara Shuddou Sattva shuddihi sattva shuddow druva smritihi (आहारशुद्धौ सत्त्वशुद्धिः, सत्त्वशुद्धौ ध्रुवा स्मृतिः) -- If ones diet is pure, mind also gets purified. Pure mind leads to sharp memory - Chandognya Upanishad. Have food that is succulent, juicy, nourishing, pleasing to the heart and satwic (pure) in quality so that it promotes life, vitality, strength, health, happiness and satisfaction.
  12. It is said that those who know the art of eating are disease free. Food is the best medicine. Emphasis is laid on chewing food well. When food is chewed well, it produces sufficient saliva, which in turn helps in digestion. There are some famous sayings about how we should eat. ‘Chew your food as your stomach has no teeth’. ‘Taste your food, your stomach has no taste buds’. ‘Eat to live and not, live to eat’.
  13. We should know how much to eat. It is said that our stomach should be half full with solids, quarter full with water and a quarter should be empty. The quantity of food we eat depends on the type of work we do. The Vasista Smriti and Varaha Purana say ekadashyam upavaso yam nirmitam paramoushadam (एकादश्यामुपवासोऽयं निर्मितं परमौषधम् ।) -- ‘fasting once a fortnight on the day of ekadashi (the 11th day after a full moon and new moon) is the best medicine.’ Fasting rests the digestive system, digestive fluids get regenerated, and the mind becomes fresh and clear.
  14. Avoid food that is spicy, salty, hot, sour, which ultimately produces grief as they make the mind restless (rajasic).
  15. Stay away from food that is stale, without nutrients, smelly or partially eaten by others as they make our mind dull (tamasic).
  16. Avoid coffee, tea, carbonated drinks and intoxicants.
  17. Avoid non vegetarian food. Consume easily digestible vegetarian food.
  18. Do not consume food when you are upset, angry or in fear.
  19. Do not eat when you are not hungry.
  20. Do not eat soon after your bowel evacuation.
  21. Have food at least half an hour after Surya Namaskara.
  22. Pregnant women and women during the monthly cycle should avoid Surya Namaskara as it applies pressure on the abdomen.
  23. Be in the company of noble people.
  24. Read scriptures daily and reflect on its inner meaning instead of being satisfied with the outer meaning.
  25. Allocate some time for yourself and to meditate on the Lord daily.

The 16 Surya Namaskara steps

1. Sama Stiti (Relax)

Meaning:
Sama Stiti means relaxed and balanced position.

Benefits:
Improves the alertness.

Process:

  • Stand upright with big toes together. Bring your palms in to anjali mudra with thumbs touching the heart center
  • Body weight is equally distributed on both feet
  • Chin up and look ahead.

2. Urdwasana

Meaning:
Urdwa means up. Urdwa means upward flowing energy.

Benefits:
This asana stretches the whole body, flexes the spinal column, destresses the shoulders and neck muscles. This asana opens up the lungs and deepens the breathing.

Process:

  • Stand upright
  • With inhale (puraka) stretch your arms as back as you can with your elbows straight and palms together
  • Bring your biceps closer to your ears
  • Look at your palms.

3. Uttanasana

Meaning:
Uttanasana means stretched, intense.

Benefits:
This asana strengthens the calf muscles, hamstrings, buttocks, and flexes the spinal column, and tones the shoulder and neck muscles. This asana is helpful for stomach and uterus related ailments. It is also helpful for weight loss/obesity and slimming. People with “navel displacement” problem can be cured by this asana

Process:

  • As you exhale (rechaka) bring your palms on the ground next to the feet
  • Palms with fingers spread apart are in line with the feet
  • Keep your knees straight and bring your forehead to the knees.

4. Eka Pada Prasaranasana

Meaning:
Eka pada Prasaranasana means one legged extension.

Benefits:
This asana strengthens the hip region, leg muscles, abdominal muscles and toes.

Process:

  • Retain your palms with widespread fingers as it is and with inhalation (puraka) stretch out your right leg as back as you can and bring your right big toe on the ground.
  • Bring your left calf muscle and hamstring together
  • Push your left heel down while keeping your fingers spread apart
  • Bring your right knee on the ground.

5. Hanumasana

Meaning:
Hanuman means one who has beautiful jaws. Hanuman is the incarnation of Vayu deva and is a Foremost devotee of Lord Shri Ram.

Benefits:
This asana strengthens the back muscles, buttocks, hips and leg muscles. This destresses shoulders, spinal column and opens up the lungs

Process:

  • Holding the breath inside (antar kumbaka), raise your arms, bringing the palms together, keeping the elbows straight and biceps touching your ears.
  • Bring your biceps to your ears
  • Keep left calf muscle and hamstring together
  • Push your left heel down
  • Stretch back as much as you can
  • Look at your palms.

6. Eka Pada Prasaranasana

Meaning:
Eka pada Prasaranasana means one legged extension.

Benefits:
This asana strengthens the hip region, leg muscles, abdominal muscles and toes.

Process:

  • With exhalation (rechaka) bring your palms to the ground on either side of the left foot and spread your fingers wide apart
  • Keep your elbows straight.
  • Bring your left calf muscle and hamstring together
  • Push your left heel down and spread your fingers apart.

7. Dwi Pada Prasaranasana

Meaning:
Dwi pada Prasaranasana means both legged extension.

Benefits:
This asana strengthens the hands, shoulders, back, hip region, leg muscles, abdominal muscles and toes.

Process:

  • With inhalation (puraka) stretch out the other leg also and bring both big toes in one line
  • Bring both knees above the ground and keep your back straight
  • Keep your elbows straight and look ahead
  • Body weight is distributed on the big toes and palms.

8. Shashankasana

Meaning:
Shashanka means rabbit. Shashi means mind.

Benefits:
This asana energises the abdominal muscles, squeezes the lower and middle lungs and activates the upper lungs. This asana relaxes the mind, stretches the shoulder and back muscles, increases blood flow to the brain and energizes degenerated brain cells. People with migraine problem find this beneficial.

Process:

  • With exhalation (rechaka) bring your buttocks on your heels and your forehead on the ground
  • Keep your elbows straight.
  • Bring your chest on your thighs
  • Bring big toes together
  • Do not move your palms and toes.

9. Sastanga Namaskara:

Meaning:
Skanda Purarana defines sastanga namaskara as

Urasa shirasa dristya
manasa vachasa tata
padbhyam karabhyam janubhyam
pranamo astanga reeritaha

उरसा शिरसा दृष्ट्या मनसा वचसा तथा ।
पद्भ्यां कराभ्यां जानुभ्यां प्रणामोऽष्टाङ्ग ईरितः ।।

Mind is full of reverence, eyes are full of devotion, mouth is glorifying Him, while head, hands, chest, thighs and feet are at the Feet of Him.

Hold the breath out (Bahya kumbhaka) mind, eyes, mouth in praise of the Lord and forehead, palms, chest, knees, and feet on the ground is astanga namaskara (complete 8 limbed surrender). We are not holding anything within and even the air we have exhaled. In yoga this is bhakti, sharanagati or devotion.

Benefits:
This asana relaxes the mind, strengthens the hands. People with migraine problem finds this beneficial.

Process:

  • Holding the breath out (bahya kumbaka) stretch your body forward
  • Bring your forehead, chest and knees on the ground and elbows up
  • Do not move your palms and toes.

10. Urdwa Mukha Swanasana

Meaning:
Urdwa mukha means upward turned face. Swana means dog

Benefits:
This asana helps people with asthma and bronchitis and shoulder, back and thyroid problems. This activates thyroid, thymus and pineal glands

Process:

  • With inhalation (puraka) raise the upper body up to the navel and stretch neck as far back as you can.
  • Feel the flexing of your spinal column, stretch in your neck and shoulder muscles and opening of the chest muscles
  • Keep the big toes together and do not move your toe and palm positions.

11. Ado Mukha Swanasana

Meaning:
Ado mukha means face turned downward. Swana means dog.

Benefits:
This asana strengthens the arms, and legs, energises the brain cells, internal organs, spinal column, kidneys and liver, releases tension from the shoulder and back muscles and helps stretch the hamstring muscles, calf muscles and Achilles tendons. This energizes the kidneys and abdominal muscles.

It purifies the blood, improves memory, decompresses the internal organs, rehydrates the discs in the spine, slows down the heart rate, massages the heart muscles and oxygenates the blood. People with cholesterol problem will find this asana beneficial.

Process:

  • With exhalation (rechaka) raise your body up into the mountain posture.
  • The elbows should be straight with the fingers spread wide apart
  • Straighten the knees and push your tailbone up
  • Push the crown of your head towards the ground
  • Bring your heels on the ground
  • Feel the stretch on your shoulders, back, buttocks, hamstring, calf and Achilles tendons
  • Bring your big toes together.

12. Shashankasana

Meaning:
Shashanka means rabbit. Shashi means mind.

Benefits:
This asana energises the abdominal muscles, squeezes the lower and middle lungs and activates the upper lungs. This asana relaxes the mind, stretches the shoulder and back muscles, increases blood flow to the brain and energizes degenerated brain cells. People with migraine problem finds this beneficial.

Process:

  • Holding the breath out (bhahya kumbhaka) bring your buttocks on your heels and bring your forehead on the ground
  • Keep your elbows straight
  • Bring your chest on your thighs
  • Bring big toes together
  • Do not move your palms and toes.

13. Eka Pada Prasaranasana

Meaning:
Eka pada Prasaranasana means one legged extension.

Benefits:
This asana strengthens the hip region, leg muscles, abdominal muscles and toes.

Process:

  • With inhalation (puraka) bring your right foot between your palms
  • Bring your right calf muscle and hamstring together
  • Push your right heel down and spread your fingers apart
  • Keep your elbows straight.

14. Hanumasana

Meaning:
Hanuman means one who has beautiful jaws. Hanuman is the incarnation of Vayu deva and is a great devotee of Lord Shri Ram.

Benefits:
This asana strengthens the back muscles, buttocks, hips and leg muscles. This destresses shoulders, spinal column and opens up the lungs.

Process:

  • Holding the breath inside (antar kumbaka) raise your arms and bring the palms together keeping elbows straight
  • Bring your biceps to your ears
  • Keep right calf muscle and hamstring together
  • Push your right heel down
  • Stretch back as much as you can
  • Look at your palms.

15. Eka Pada Prasaranasana

Meaning:
Eka pada Prasaranasana means one legged extension.

Benefits:
This asana strengthens the hip region, leg muscles, abdominal muscles and toes.

Process:

  • With exhalation (rechaka) bring your palms to the ground on either side of the right foot and spread your fingers wide apart.
  • Keep your right calf muscle and hamstring together.
  • Push your right heel down and spread your fingers apart
  • Keep your elbows straight.

16. Uttanasana

Meaning:
Uttanasana means stretched, intense.

Benefits:
This asana strengthens the calf muscles, hamstrings, buttocks, flexes the spinal column, tones the shoulder and neck muscles. This asana is helpful for stomach and uterus related ailments. It is also helpful for weight loss/obesity and slimming. People with “navel displacement” problem can be cured by this asana.

Process:

  • Holding the breath out (bahya kumbhaka) bring your palms on the ground next to the feet
  • Bring your feet and palms in one line with fingers spread apart
  • Keep your knees straight and bring your forehead to the knees.

1.Sama Stiti (Relax)

Benefits:
Improves the alertness.

Process:

  • With inhalation stand upright with big toes together. Bring your palms in to anjali mudra with thumbs touching the heart center
  • Body weight is equally distributed on both feet
  • Chin up and look ahead.
