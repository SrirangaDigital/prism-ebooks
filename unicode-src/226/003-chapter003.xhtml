Mudra and Bandha

Mudra means symbol. Mudra means insignia. Mudra means commitment. Samudra means full commitment. That is why God is called as Samudra - as He has full commitment in everything and never does anything half-heartedly.

According to Gheranda Samhita, Mudras and Bandhas are done to steady the body. Mudras and Bandhas help in preventing the dissipation of energy, and help in redirecting the energy. Bandhas are the lock and mudras are the lock over a lock.

Hata Yoga Pradipika talks about Maha Mudra, Maha Bandha, Maha Vedha, Jalandhar Bandha, Uddyan Bandha, Moola Bandha, Viparta Karini Mudra, Khechari Mudra, Aswini Mudra, Vrajoli Mudra, Shambhavi Mudra and Shan Mukhi Mudra.

Ha means sun, Ta means moon. The seat of moon centre is the Sahasrara chakra, and the seat of sun system is the manipura chakra at the navel. Our active life continues as long as the nectar remains in the sahasrara chakra at the crown. Just like pressing the accelerator in the car draws more fuel, when we breathe rapidly, we draw more nectar from the sahasrara chakra which flows into the manipura chakra where it gets consumed. Bandhas like Jalandhara bandha, Kriyas like Vasudeva Kriya, Viparita Karani, asanas such as Shirasasana, Sarvangasana, Vrischikasana etc. reduce the consumption of the nectar (amruta) at Manipura chakra and preserve the body's vitality.

By manipulating our prana or vital energy, we can direct it towards better health, well being and personal transformation.

Mudra is defined as Mudham Dadaati Devaanam Dravayasya Asuraarapi -- ‘That by which the divine beings get happiness and the negative forces and diseases are driven away’. Mudras are used in Tantra puja.

Mudras are classified in to:

  • Kaya Mudra (mudra through body- Viparitakarani, Yoga mudra, Manduki Mudra etc)
  • Mana Mudra (mudra through the senses on the face- using lips, eyes, nose, ear, tongue -- Shambhavi, Kaki, Akashi, Nasika Dristi, Shanmukhi, Khechari)
  • Bandha (Jaalandhar, Uddiyan, Moola and Maha)
  • Adhara Mudra (supporting mudras - Aswini, Vrajoli etc)
  • Hasta Mudra (mudras through hands - balance the
    • Mahabhootas like fire, water, air, earth and ether elements and
    • Tanmaatras like taste, touch, smell and sound in the body and
    • Doshas in the body like kapha (phlegm), pitta (fire) and vata (air).

Dhanvantari Mantra (Healing mantra):

Om Ayam me hasto Bhagavan ayam me bhagavattaraha
Ayam me viswa bheshajaha ayam Shivabhi marshanaha

ॐ अयं मे हस्तो भगवानयं मे भगवत्तरः । अयं मे विश्ववेदसोऽयं शिवाऽऽभिमर्शनः ।

The broad meaning of this mantra: “The Supreme Lord resides in my palms as the greatest healer. All the medicinal herbs of this world are in my palms. By His Grace, through these palms I heal myself and I will be an instrument in healing others.”

The body is made up of 5 elements -- Earth, Water, Fire, Air and Ether. When these five elements are not in balance we get various physical and psychological ailments. The energy from the body radiates out mainly through the tip of the nose, lips, fingertips and toes. When the thumb is brought in contact with the other fingers, energy is directed back to vitalize various parts of our body. This is called a circuit bypass. Mudras and bandhas go hand in hand. Bandhas help in locking in the energy, whereas mudras help in redirecting the energy.

Mudras are the expression of our inner feeling. In the Tantra shastra, Mudra is defined as ‘the one which gives happiness.’ Mudras form a part of yoga and are together referred to as Yoga Mudra. Mudras done using hands are called Hasta Mudra. Indian classical dances such as Bharata Natyam, Yakshagana, Mohini Aattam, Kathakali, Kuchupudi, Oddissi make use of mudras to express their art forms. In the temples deities show different mudras. Of those the most well-known mudra is the abhaya mudra (reassuring mudra). In Tirupathi, Balaji’s statue depicts that ‘if you surrender to my feet, your problems are knee deep - and you can easily walk through them.’

The five fingers represent the five elements (tatvas):

  • Thumb: Fire (agni)
  • Index Finger: Air (vayu)
  • Middle finger: Space (akasha)
  • Ring Finger: Earth (pritvi)
  • Small finger: Water (jala)

Fire is the most important of the five elements. When the fire element becomes weak in the body, various ailments come to the body. Through the practice of Mudras the fire element (thumb) is joined with the other elements (other fingers) to vitalize those elements also.

3. Khechari Mudra

Meaning:
Kha means space. Chara means move. Here we are moving the tongue in the throat space. Kha also means bliss. In Vedas it is said somam pibet na suram pibet (सोमं पिबेत् न सुरां पिबेत्) -- Drink the Soma rasa (nectar) and do not drink alcohol. We can drink the soma rasa produced within by doing khechari mudra.

There is a mantra in Veda which says Om Vam Vartu mandala madyasta sweta varno Varunantargata Shri Vasudeva mat sharramastam Amruta Vristya Plavayatu (ॐ वं वर्तुलमण्डलमध्यस्थः श्वेतवर्णः वरुणान्तर्गतश्रीवासुदेवः मच्छरीरं अमृतवृष्ट्या आप्लावयतु ।।) -- In the crown centre amidst of the white hue the indrewller of Varuna may the Lord Shri Vasudeva nourish all the limbs of the body with amruta (nectar).

Moon centre in Sahsrara chakra (crown chakra) produces Amruta (soma rasa -- nectar). It gets wasted when it falls and gets consumed in the sun centre (Manipurs chakra).

In the mouth, there are two dangling bits -- one is tongue and another is inner tongue called uvula. Uvula looks like an udder. We are using the tongue to milk the amruta (nectar).

  • Hata Yoga Pradipika says One should saturate the body from head to foot with nectar (flowing from moon). Then he becomes endowed with excellent body, great strength and valor. - khechari is explained in 4-53
  • If the sushumna is stopped without the suspension of prana, it perfects the khechari. By frequent practice of kechar, unmani follows. 4-47
  • One should practice khechari until one goes in to yoga Samadhi. In this sleep, time (death) does not exist 4-49

Benefits:
By the practice of this mudra the yogi becomes free from fainting, hunger, thirst and laziness. He or she becomes free from diseases, decay, old age and death. This mudra makes one an urdhvaretas (one in whom the energy flows upward). As the body of the yogi is filled with nectar, he will have resistance even to virulent poison. You feel the sweetness in the throat and bliss increases. This mudra gives siddhis (powers) to Yogis. This mudra helps in transcending the mind called unmani bhava. Khechari is the best of all mudras.

Process:

  • Sit in Siddasana, Padmasana, Ardha Padmasana or Vajrasana.
  • If you cannot sit on the floor you can sit on a chair.
  • Keep your body, head and neck in one line.
  • Hold the chin up.
  • Bring the thumb and index finger together in chin mudra on your knees. 
  • Inhale and raise your tongue up and take it as back as you can.
  • Stretch your neck up as back as you can as if you are looking at the ceiling in to Akashi Mudra.
  • Stretch your tongue further back so as to touch the root of the upper palate to catch the nectar trickling from the moon centre.
  • Feel the stretch in the lower tendon of the tongue.
  • This slows down the breathing.
  • Remain here for as long as you can. Exhale and relax. Refer to pictures 71 and 72.

Caution:
People with neck injury should do this mudra with caution.

7. Shanmukhi Mudra

Meaning:
The Hata Yoga Pradipika says that by sitting in siddasana and by assuming the Shambhavi mudra, one should listen with a concentrated mind to the inner sound. This is heard in the right ear.

Shanmukhi mudra is a preparation to hear this inner sound. Close the ears, the nose, the mouth, and the eyes. Then a clear sound is heard in the Sushumna whch is purified by pranayama. 4-68

Shanmukha means six faces- eyes, ears, nose mouth, skin and mind.

Benefits:
This mudra helps in pratyahara, dhyana and in hearing the anahata nada (inner non-vibratory sound). The mind becomes introverted.

Process:

  • Hold the chin up.
  • Close the ears with the thumbs.
  • Close the eyes with the index fingers.
  • Close nostrils partially with the middle fingers.
  • Pull the upper lips down with the ring fingers.
  • Push the lower lips up with the little fingers.
  • Breathe gently.
  • Hear the inner sound and dive deep into meditation. Refer to picture 74.
  • Hold this position for as long as you can. Relax and repeat the same and do as long as you can.
