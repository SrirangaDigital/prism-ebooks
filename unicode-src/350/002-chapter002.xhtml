The height of candles may differ but they yield the same brightness and light. It’s not our position or ability that actually makes us shine, it’s our attitude.

Anon

Your living is determined not so much by what life brings to you as by the attitude you bring to life; not so much by what happens to you as by the way your mind looks at what happens.

Kahlil Gibran

Problem is common to all. But attitude makes all the difference. When it rains, all birds occupy shelter, but eagle is the only bird, which avoids the rain, by flying above the clouds

Nandita looked very ‘cool’ in her beige colour sari that Sunday when the group met her. ‘Self-esteem is how we look at ourselves. All of us live in this society and we encounter people and situations day in and day out. Our surroundings invoke certain emotions in us which result in our behaviour in the society, towards people and situations. The interesting fact is that these emotions are controlled by what we call our ‘beliefs’ or ‘attitudes’. In the course of our life, we develop attitudes towards our surroundings, people and life in general. Attitudes can be positive or negative. Negative attitude results in making us, as well as people around us unhappy. If we take things positively we can cope with everything that comes our way easily. The next topic we are going to discuss is ‘positive attitude’. I would like us to begin with a small task’. Nandita took out a sheet of paper. All of them read the instructions.

Anjali was the quickest to give the answers. ‘Hard work is 98 Knowledge is 96 and attitude is 100 percent’.

‘So, what we can conclude is, though hard work and knowledge do contribute a lot to success it is the attitude which matters’ Nandita commented.

‘I can understand this. Unless we have the right attitude we neither work hard nor do we make an effort to acquire knowledge’. Raju said.

Tejas asked, ‘How does one develop positive attitude?’

‘Well, positive attitude manifests in your thinking, let us say, in your creative and constructive thinking. Also, it helps in developing optimism, to be motivated to accomplish goals. In general, it gives happiness, spreads happiness’. Nandita paused.

‘Is it like looking at a glass of water and saying whether it is half full or half empty?’ Ashish grinned.

‘Hmm, yes. This is a very common example we take to illustrate attitude’. Nandita said.

‘So, our attitude is responsible for giving us confidence in our abilities to solve problems’. Raju observed.

‘Absolutely. It includes attitude to self, attitude towards others which is responsible for the interpersonal relationship, attitude towards profession and attitude towards change. In a way our attitude helps in our career and shapes our life’. Nandita affirmed.

‘Aren’t we born with our attitudes? Can we ‘develop’ attitude or change it?’ Swapna expressed her doubt.

‘Well, it is a part of our personality. But there is always a scope to change our attitude. Exposure to life, reading good literature, learning from great people and how they have dealt with problems—all these certainly help us develop a positive attitude’. Nandita concluded the discussion with one more piece of information.

‘Do you know that at Harvard, one of the most prestigious universities in the world, the most popular and successful course teaches you how to learn to be happier? The Positive Psychology class taught by Tal Ben Shahar attracts 1400 students per semester and 20% of Harvard graduates take this elective course. This course is about the psychological aspects of life fulfilment and flourishing, by teaching about empathy, friendship, love, achievement, creativity, spirituality, happiness and humour. According to Tal Ben Shahar, the class - which focuses on happiness, self-esteem and motivation - gives students the tools to succeed and face life with more joy. He said, ‘Attaining lasting happiness requires that we enjoy the journey on our way toward a destination we deem valuable. Happiness, therefore, is not about making it to the peak of the mountain, nor is it about climbing aimlessly around the mountain: happiness is the experience of climbing toward the peak’

‘He also gave five positive tips that work in building happiness. They are:

  1. accept painful emotions
  2. spend quality time with people we love and care for
  3. do physical exercise
  4. cultivate the habit of gratitude
  5. simplify, do less work and increase the quality of work that will enhance productivity.

UBUNTU - A very nice story from Africa ...

The motivation behind the Ubuntu culture in Africa ...

An Anthropologist proposed a game to the African tribal children. He placed a basket of sweets near a tree and made the children stand 100 metres away. Then he announced that whoever reaches first would get all the sweets in the basket. When he said ‘ready steady go!’

Do you know what these children did?

They all held each other’s hands, ran together towards the tree, divided the sweets equally among themselves, ate the sweets and enjoyed it.

When the Anthropologist asked them why they did so, they answered ‘Ubuntu’.

Which meant -
‘How can one be happy when the others are sad?’
Ubuntu in their language means -
‘I am because we are!’

‘This is an excellent message of achieving collective success. This message is given in one of the corporate games played to build attitude to success. ‘No work is inferior. Whatever we do we must excel in it. This episode from Abraham Lincoln’s life tells us this’. Amina read out what she brought.

On the first day, as President, Abraham Lincoln entered to give his inaugural address, just in the middle, one man stood up. He was a rich aristocrat. He said, ‘Mr. Lincoln, you should not forget that your father used to make shoes for my family’. And the whole Senate laughed; they thought they had made fun of Abraham Lincoln.

Lincoln looked at the man and said, ‘Sir I know that my father used to make shoes in your house for your family and many others here…. Because the way he made shoes; nobody else could. He was a creator. His shoes were not just shoes; he poured his whole soul in it. I want to ask you, have you any complaint? Because I know how to make shoes myself. If you have any complaint I can make another pair of shoes. But as far as I know, nobody has ever complained about my father’s shoes. He was a genius, a great creator and I am proud of my father’.

The whole Senate was struck dumb. They could not understand what kind of man Abraham Lincoln was. He was proud because his father did the job so well that not even a single complaint had ever been heard.

‘This also speaks for the self-esteem of Lincoln’. Amina commented.

All these stories were placed on the college notice board. This time the group found that the students were discussing the stories in the canteen over a cup of tea!
