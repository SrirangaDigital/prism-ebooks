The Model of PD

The word ‘dialogue’ is rooted in the Greek words ‘dia’ (through) and ‘logos’ (words); in a broad sense, it implies, to make meaning through an exchange of words. The shared understanding of ‘words’ and their significance is one of the most important cultural factors that bind us as a community.

I believe that the journey of a ‘Dialogue’ to a ‘Powerful Dialogue’ is a sacred journey - a journey facilitated by ‘Intent’ and ‘Hope’ on the part of the stakeholders, and the chief stakeholders being the ‘sender’ and ‘receiver’ (sender implies counsellor/trainer/therapist and receiver implies counselled/trainee/client).

Intent

INTENT is the purpose with which both sender and receiver enter into a dialogue. ‘Intent’ of a dialogue binds the sender and receiver. The catalyst that transforms a mere dialogue into a powerful dialogue is the intent to facilitate transformation on the part of the sender and the intent to evolve on the part of the receiver.

When the ‘purpose’ of a Dialogue is to ‘facilitate’ the experience of ‘introspection’ that can lead to ‘transformation’, it becomes therapeutic and adds a new dimension to one’s personality.

Sincerity of purpose distinguishes powerful dialogue from dialogue, conversation, argu ment, debate and discussion. Every verbal exchange carries implicit and/or explicit purposes - to prove oneself right, to contra dict, to persuade, to manipulate, to malign, to coerce, to inform, among others. Sincerity of purpose brings authenticity to the whole conversation, and thus it becomes an experiential journey.

An intent of, ‘I will change your life’ is a false and patronising approach. I strongly believe that nobody can change another’s life. The only genuine intent is, ‘I can assist you in change’. I firmly believe that it is the sacred moral responsibility of the sender to help create a transformative journey for the receiver.

Hope

The receiver may or may not be equipped with the intent to change, or may or may not believe in the possibility or scope for change. This is when the sender has to create HOPE in the receiver that change is possible.

Hope implies the presence of a certain amount of despair, suffering or perseverance - i.e., believing that a better or positive outcome is possible even when there is evidence to the contrary. Hope contrasts with wishing, dreaming and desiring, which imply urgent ego claims and controls aimed at particular objects and goals. Hope is an arduous search for a future good of some kind that is realistically possible but not yet visible. Hope is tied with imagination - imagining that what we really need is possible, though difficult.

Hoping is a realistic and adaptive response to extreme stress or crisis situations whereby an individual acquires a patient and confident surrender to uncontrollable forces. However, hope is not initiated and sustained by erasing emotions of fear and anxiety but by integrating the realistic obstacles and threats that exist, into the proposed coping and transformation strategies. Hope brings reality into sharp focus. Hope incorporates fear into the process of rational deliberation and tempers it.

Hope helps transcend experiences of despair, distress and pain. When the receiver believes or is convinced or committed to hope, the sender and receiver unknowingly create a path of journey for the receiver. In creating a path for the receiver, the sender also traverses the path of self-discovery.

Hope is, therefore, a thin thread that holds the morale of the receiver. When you create hope, you create a series of positive vibes and energy in the receiver to do things. Hope creates a set of possibilities - possibilities of a beautiful world, of peace and happiness and ultimately leads to faith.

Armed with hope and positivity, the receiver is empowered to find hitherto unknown paths and solutions. Once intent and hope are articulated and created, the situation is most conducive for transformation.

Intent and hope:

* Establish mutual understanding.

* Create an open environment.

* Establish mutual trust.

* Establish mutual respect and acceptance.

* Build awareness about the sincerity of purpose.

With hope, the intent to transform is also created in the receiver. The intent of the receiver is a precondition for change since change is a profoundly personal experience.

Personal Journey for Both

The sender can only create a conducive environment, but the journey of transformation has to be undertaken by the receiver only.

One of the most significant insights that I had during the journey of this book is that I, as a sender, have myself experienced transformation with each receiver. The journey of dialogue to powerful dialogue is analogous with the journey of internal transformation of both the speaker and receiver.

Tool of Introspection

You would have noticed that each receiver (including myself) travelled ‘within’ to understand his/her own self rather than to understand others. Each receiver also found resolution through a better understanding of oneself. Each dialogue had personal implications for the receiver. How did this happen? We used the most powerful tool available to mankind - INTROSPECTION.

There is always an element of introspection, learning and realization, however small it may be. Thus, the person is the content. The content or tool that sparks change is found within each of us. Harnessing this internal energy is the key to change.

Context

Is Introspection sufficient? Or do we need more? Introspection invariably happens in a CONTEXT; it arises out of the specificities of a context. A powerful dialogue can be replicated in different contexts, but the outcomes and implications may vary. One of the reasons why many dialogues fail to make an impact is the mismatch between intent, context and dialogue, and the lack of a shared understanding of the same between the sender and receiver.

Metaphors, quotes, idioms, proverbs or words randomly thrown at us have the potential to take on the mantle of becoming Powerful Dialogues. However, transformation can occur only when a dialogue touches a deep chord inside the receiver, and it is in this connection that we find a new perspective or a new meaning. This leads to increased awareness of the intent, and hope is generated. The dialogue then takes on power and becomes a Powerful Dialogue.

Moment of Epiphany

Once the relationship between the intent, context and dialogue is articulated clearly, then the dialogue begins, and it is in this process that one or both participants experience the WOW or FROZEN moment of self-realization.
