Shat Karma Kriya

Six ways of purifying the body

Neti | Kapalabhati | Douti | Basti | Nauli | Tratak

Shat Karma Kriya explains the six ways of purifying the body as mentioned in Hata Yoga Pradipika and Gheranda Samhita. To be successful in our sadhana, we need to purify ourselves. These purification techniques remove gross impurities from the body and improve the health. By doing the Shat Karma Kriyas, we feel lightness in the body and mind. The Shat Karma Kriyas are Neti, Kapalabhati, Douti, Basti, Nauli and Tratak.

Neti

The two types of Neti Kriyas are Jala Neti and Sutra Neti.

⚈ Jala Neti, Type 1

➲ Meaning

Jala means water. Neti means nasal passage.

☞Benefits

People with migraine and hay fever problems find this kriya beneficial This kriya improves eye sight

⚙ Process

  1. Prepare lukewarm saline water and fill a neti pot.
  2. Bend the head sideways. Use the neti pot to pour the solution into one nostril.
  3. The entire sinus region gets cleansed and water comes out through the other nostril.
  4. Repeat the same on the other side. Please see Picture 1 and YouTube www.youtube.com/watch?v=SWz9PreLwqE
  5. Blow your nostrils few times followed by a few rounds of kapalabhati to remove any trace of moisture.

⚠ Caution

People with ear ache should do this kriya under supervision.

⚈ Sutra Neti, Type 2

➲ Meaning

Sutra means thread or catheter. Sutra Neti is more intense than Jala Neti.

☞Benefits

This kriya improves eyesight and is beneficial for people with migraine, hay fever and heavily blocked nostrils.

⚙ Process

  1. Moisten the rubber catheter.
  2. Slowly insert the smooth end of the catheter into one nostril.
  3. When it comes through the throat, slowly pull it out through the mouth.
  4. Repeat the same on the other nostril. Please see Picture 2.
  5. Blow out through both nostrils a few times and do a few rounds of kapalabhati.

⚠ Caution

While performing this kriya, it is normal for some people to bleed from the nose or for tears to flow. Beginners should do this kriya under supervision.

⚈ Kapalabhati

➲ Meaning

Kapala means skull. Bhati means illuminate.

☞Benefits

This pranayama increases glow in the face, activates brain cells, improves memory, strengthens and balances the nervous system, relaxes the mind, purifies the nadis, heats up the body and helps in burning fat. It also cleanses the sinus, increases the lung capacity and helps people with breathing disorders such as asthma.

⚙ Process

  1. Sit in Siddasana, Padmasana, Ardha Padmasana or Vajrasana as shown in Pictures 159â€“162.
  2. If you cannot sit on the floor you can sit on a chair.
  3. Keep your body, head and neck in one line.
  4. Hold your chin up and keep your hands on the knees.
  5. Lean forward slightly to keep your spine erect.
  6. Push the air out through the nostrils and simultaneously pull the stomach in.
  7. Do not push the air out very hard and ensure that the shoulders are steady throughout the practice.
  8. Do not perform active inhalation. Kapalabhati is active exhalation with passive inhalation.
  9. Do kapalabhati for a minute and relax. Repeat 3 times.

⚠ Caution

Stop Kapalabhati pranayama if you feel tired or giddy. This is not for people with heart ailments or very high blood pressure. Doing kapalabhati for too long at one stretch may wear out the internal organs. Seek expert advice before doing Kalapabhati.

Douti

➲ Meaning

Douti means washing. The six doutis are described below.

⚈ Danta Douti

☞Benefits

This kriya energises the tongue, increases life span and keeps teeth and gums healthy.

⚙ Process

  1. Massage your gums using your thumb and fore finger.
  2. Massage the upper and lower palates.
  3. Rinse your mouth well with water.

⚠ Caution

People with ear ache should do this kriya under supervision.

⚈ Vaman Douti

☞Benefits

This kriya cleanses the esophagus, removes phlegm and excess bile from the system and brings relief in the case of over-eating or consumption of unpleasant food.

⚙ Process

  1. Early in the morning on an empty stomach drink two cups of lukewarm water quickly.
  2. Using the fore, middle and ring fingers, tickle the root of your tongue to induce vomiting.
  3. All the water you drank along with phlegm and excess bile gushes out through the mouth.

⚠ Caution

Beginners should do this kriya under supervision.

⚈ Jihva Douti

➲ Meaning

Jihva means tongue.

☞Benefits

According to Gheranda Samhita -by elongating the tongue, one destroys old age problems and all diseases.

⚙ Process

  1. Put the index, middle and ring fingers of the right hand into the throat and rub the root of the tongue thoroughly. Throw out any resulting phlegm.
  2. Get hold of the tongue with thumb, index and middle fingers of both hands and pull the tongue out as much as you can. This helps in elongating the tongue. Please refer to Pictures 3 and 4.

⚈ Karna Douti

➲ Meaning

Karna means ears.

⚈ Vastra Douti

➲ Meaning

Vastra means cloth.

☞Benefits

This kriya promotes good health and cheerfulness. It cures abdominal and skin diseases, fever, enlarged spleen, bile and phlegm disorders.

⚙ Process

  1. Squat and be comfortable.
  2. Prepare a saline solution in a bowl. Add a pinch of turmeric powder to it to make it antiseptic.
  3. Dip a rolled cotton bandage in this solution.
  4. Slowly unroll the bandage and start swallowing it from one end.
  5. With the fore, middle fingers and thumbs hold on to the bandage at the other end.
  6. When you have swallowed about two metres of bandage, slowly pull it out.
  7. You will see extra phlegm and bile coming out. Please refer to Picture 7.

⚠ Caution

Beginners should do this kriya under supervision.

Basti

➲ Meaning

Basti means bladder. Basti kriya cleanses the esophagus and the stomach down to the rectum. Two types of basti mentioned here are Jala Basti and Shankaprakshalana.

⚈ Jala Basti

➲ Meaning

Jala means water.

☞Benefits

Basti kriya cleanses the intestine downwards. People with constipation, urinary disorders, flatulence and piles related problems will find this beneficial.

⚙ Process

  1. Stand in clear, pure water up to the navel.
  2. Open and close your anus with Aswini mudra.
  3. This sucks the water in through the anus and cleanses the large intestine.
  4. Using the middle finger, clean the anus and lower part of the large intestine.

⚠ Caution

Beginners should do this under guidance.

⚈ Shanka Prakshalana

➲ Meaning

Shanka means conch and here it indicates the stomach which looks like a conch shell. Prakshalana means thorough washing. The stomach that resembles a conch shell gets a thorough washing with this kriya.

☞Benefits

This kriya alleviates digestive problems, tones the liver and other digestive organs and glands. It strengthens the immune system, reduces excessive mucus and purifies the blood. It recharges the entire pranic body, cleanses the nadis, raises energy levels and helps with inner sadhana.

⚙ Process

  1. Mix one tea spoon of rock salt in one litre of lukewarm water.
  2. Drink two glasses of this solution.
  3. Then in sequence do 3 rounds of Tadasana, Triyak Tadasana, Ardhakati Chakrasana, Parivritta Trikonasana, Triyak Bhujangasana and Udara Karshasana. Please refer to Pictures 8 to 16 for these asanas.
  4. Drink 2 more glasses of the above solution and repeat the process.
  5. At the end go to the toilet and do Aswini mudra by moving the anus in and out to stimulate the peristaltic movement.
  6. Spend a minute in the toilet. The aim is to build internal cleansing pressure.
  7. At first solid stool is evacuated, followed by a mixture of stool and water.
  8. As the practice progresses, more water and less solid stool will be evacuated.
  9. Eventually yellow water and finally clear water will be evacuated.
  10. The whole process takes about 1.5 to 2 hours.
  11. Some may not pass stool for 2â€“3 days after this, but this is normal.

Subsequent Kriyas

After Shanka Prakshalana, do Douti to remove excess water from the system. Also do Jala Neti followed by Kapalabati to avoid getting a headache. Do Agnisara Kriya and do Yoga Nidra for 45 minutes.

Food Restriction

For one month, after the practise of Shanka Prakshalana kriya, all chemically processed, synthetic, pungent, spicy, acidic, rich and non-vegetarian food must be strictly avoided including pickles, chocolates, sweets, ice cream and soft drinks. Milk, butter milk, yoghurt and all acidic fruits such as lime, grapefruit, oranges and pineapples should be avoided. Coffee, tea, alcohol and other intoxicants should not be consumed.

The diet should be pure, simple and natural. It may include food such as rice, wheat, bread, vegetables, nuts, lentils and other pulses. Use common sense when planning your diet.

Special Food

Kichari prepared with Basmati Rice and Mung Dal, cooked with ghee is ideal to eat after Shanka Prakshalana kriya. The rice and lentils should be cooked together so that the final preparation is semi-liquid.

The ghee provides the necessary coating to the intestinal walls until the body produces a new lining. The rice provides a simple, easily digestible packing material in the form of carbohydrate, and creates mucus which also protects the inner lining of the alimentary canal. Lentils supplement the diet by giving the body an easily digestible source of protein, and makes for an all-round nutritious meal.

Sufficient quantity of kichari must be eaten to keep the walls of the gut stretched, otherwise they may cramp due to the absence of bulk food. This bulk helps in the resumption of peristaltic motion and prevents indigestion, diarrhoea and constipation. No water or other fluids should be taken for at least 2 hours after the first meal.

The second meal of kichari should be taken about 6 hours after the first meal.

⚠ Caution

People with kidney problems, high blood pressure and pregnant women should avoid doing Shanka Prakshalana kriya. People with medical conditions should seek expert advice before doing this kriya. Beginners should do this kriya under supervision.

Nauli

Nauli means tube. Here stomach muscles are made into a channel or a tube. Agnisara Kriya and Uddayana Bandha are the preparation for Nauli.

Agnisara Kriya

➲ Meaning

Agni means fire. Sara means essence.

☞Benefits

This is another version of Nauli. This kriya increases the digestive fire and energy level in the body. It strengthens the abdominal muscles, increases body immunity and improves health.

⚙ Process

  1. This kriya should be done on an empty stomach.
  2. Stand up with legs apart.
  3. Bend your knees slightly. Place hands on the knees and hold the chin up.
  4. Completely stretch your tongue out, keeping the eyes wide open and looking through the third eye, forcefully breathe out through your mouth 10 times.
  5. Completely exhale and pull the stomach in and out for about 100 times without breathing. Perform long inhalation and relax.

⚠ Caution

Beginners should do this kriya under supervision.

Nauli

☞Benefits

This kriya improves health by increasing the energy level and immunity in the body. It increases the digestive fire and strengthens the abdominal muscles.

⚙ Process

  1. Do this kriya on an empty stomach.
  2. Stand up with legs apart.
  3. Bend your knees slightly, place your hands on the knees and hold your chin up.
  4. Exhale completely, pull the stomach in completely, try to move all the stomach muscles to the right and hold this position for a few seconds.
  5. Then try to move all the stomach muscles to the left and hold this position for a few seconds.
  6. Inhale and relax.
  7. Exhale completely and try to pull in both the left and right stomach muscles so that only the middle stomach muscles are pushed out. The stomach should now resemble the hood of a cobra. Hold this position as long as you can and relax. Please see Picture 17.

⚠ Caution

Beginners should do this kriya under supervision.

Tratak

➲ Meaning

Tratak means gazing.

☞Benefits

Tratak kriya is beneficial for those with insomnia problems or those with jobs requiring long hours in front of the computer. Its regular practise prevents cataract, strengthens nerves in the eyes, improves eyesight and concentration, helps with meditation and activates the Ajna Chakra.

⚙ Process

  1. Light a candle and position it in such a way that the flame is at eye level and at armâ€™s length.
  2. Sit comfortably on the ground.
  3. Close your eyes and gently open them.
  4. Gaze at the brightest part of the flame for a minute without blinking.
  5. Try not to blink in spite of tears forming

Eye Exercise

☞Benefits

In addition to Tratak kriya, the following simple exercises can be practised to strengthen the nerves in the eyes, to overcome eye related problems resulting from excessive use of computers and to improve concentration, eyesight and peripheral vision.

⚙ Process

  1. Stand up straight with your body, neck and head in one line.
  2. Hold the chin up.
  3. Extend your arms fully in front of your face so that the thumbs are side-by-side and are in line with your eyes.
  4. Gaze at the tip of the thumbs without blinking for a minute or so. Please see Picture 19.
  5. Move the hands apart and without turning the head look at the tip of the right thumb, by moving the eyeballs to the right. Please see Picture 20.
  6. Look at the tip of the left thumb by moving the eyeballs to the left without moving the head. Please see Picture 21.
  7. Raise your hands above your head with thumbs facing each other.
  8. Look at the tip of the thumbs by moving the eyeballs up, without moving the head. Please see Picture 22.
  9. Bring your hands down with thumbs facing each other.
  10. Look at the tips of the thumbs by moving the eyeballs down, without moving the head. Please see Picture 23.
