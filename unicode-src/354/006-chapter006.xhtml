Things to Know Before the Practise of Yoga

One should empty the bowel and bladder before doing asana or pranayama. It is recommended to do asana and pranayama on empty stomach or about 3â€“4 hours after the meal for good results. It is recommended that you do the sadhana (regular practise) early in the morning. If early mornings are not practical, it can be done in the evenings about 30 minutes before a meal.

Inverted asanas like shirasasana, sarvangasana and back bending asanas like chakrasana are not recommended for women during their monthly cycle time. But discomfort during the monthly cycle time can be reduced by doing the forward bending asanas like janu shirasasana, upavista konasana, badda konasana, paschimottasana, uttanasana, adhomukha swanasana, kurmasana, ardha matsyendrasana, vajrasana, veerasana etc.

Pregnant women can perform some simple asanas during the first 3 months of pregnancy but are advised not to perform twisting asanas like parivritta trikonasana, parivritta paschimottasana etc.

Warm up exercises help in loosening the body and are strongly recommended to be done before performing the asanas. Without proper warm up, the practitioner may experience muscle pulls, sprains or other injuries. The following warm up exercises help in warming up the body and making it supple.

Every body is different and everybody is different. While doing your practise do not compare yourself with others. Pain is a good indicator of when you need to stop. Extreme care should be taken while doing the practise so that you do not injure yourself. Try to synchronize your movement with breathing. The general rule is that when you squeeze your lungs you exhale, and when you open your lungs you inhale.

If you have any pre-existing medical conditions, please obtain appropriate medical advice before commencing your practise.

⚈ Abdominal Warm Up

☞Benefits

Energizes and strengthens the abdominal muscles. Warms up the body and strengthens the core.

⚙ Process

  • Lie on your back with arms stretched out.
  • Imagine you are pedalling a high cycle in the space above you.
  • When you straighten the knee, turn the foot to face the ceiling.
  • Try to bring the heels as close to the ground as possible on the downward pedal movement.
  • Repeat 25 times clockwise and 25 times anti clockwise. Please see Picture 24.
  • Relax and be aware of your breathing.

⚈ Spine Warm Up

☞Benefits

Energizes, flexes and strengthens the lower back, neck and spine.

⚙ Process

  • Bend your knees and bring both knees to the left with the left knee touching the ground.
  • At the same time, turn your neck to the right and try to touch the right ear on the ground. Look at the right hand.
  • Repeat the same on the opposite side. Repeat 3 times on both sides and relax. Please see Picture 25.

⚈ Knee, Ankle and Toe Warm Up

☞Benefits

Loosens ankle joints and toes.

⚙ Process

  • Sit on the ground with the legs stretched out. Hands beside the body on the ground with fingers pointing backwards.
  • Rotate the feet clockwise and anticlockwise 10 times and relax.
  • Place the feet slightly apart and separate your toes as much as possible and squeeze your toes as much as you can and then relax. Please see Pictures 26 and 26a.
  • Place the feet apart and simultaneously touch the ground with the big toes. Then touch the ground with small toes. Please see Pictures 27 and 28.
  • Now pull the kneecaps up and relax. Repeat 10 times.

Thigh and Leg Muscle Stretch

☞Benefits

Strengthens the thigh, leg muscles, lower back and core.

⚙ Process

  • Sit on the ground and straighten the knees. Place your hands beside the body with fingers facing backwards.
  • With inhalation raise your right leg above the ground as high as you can.
  • Feel the stretch. Remain in this position as long as you can. Please see Pictures 29 and 30.
  • Repeat the same with the left leg.
  • Repeat 10 times and relax.

Neck Warm Up

☞Benefits

Loosens the neck muscles.

⚙ Process

  • Sit on the ground and straighten the knees. Place your hands beside the body with fingers facing backwards.
  • Bend your head sideways and touch each shoulder with the ear, alternating from left to right.
  • Feel the stretch.
  • Bend your head forward, bring your chin to the collarbone and relax.
  • Slowly drop your head backwards, stretch your neck as far back as possible and relax. Please see Pictures 31 to 34.

Wrist Warm Up

☞Benefits

Loosens the wrists.

⚙ Process

  • Raise the hands and bring them parallel to the ground.
  • Turn your palms into fists by placing the thumb on the palm and wrapping your fingers around them. Rotate the fists clockwise 10 times and then anticlockwise 10 times. Please see Picture 35.

Shoulder Warm Up

☞Benefits

Loosens the wrists and the shoulder region.

⚙ Process

  • Stand up with your arms beside the body.
  • With inhalation raise the right shoulder to the ear and with exhalation lower the shoulder.
  • Repeat on both sides 10 times. Please refer to Pictures 36 and 37.

Shoulder Stretch

☞Benefits

Loosens the shoulder blades and releases tension and stress from the shoulder region. This exercise is beneficial for people with stiff or frozen shoulders.

⚙ Process

  • Sit down with your legs stretched out in front and your knees straight.
  • Place your hands beside the body with fingers pointing backwards.
  • Inhale and pull the body forward.
  • Bend your knees and try to bring calf muscles towards the hamstring without moving the hand position.
  • Bring your buttocks on ground and feel the stretch in your shoulder region.
  • Exhale and come to original position. Repeat 10 times. Please see Pictures 38 and 39.

Head and Fingers Warm Up

☞Benefits

Loosens the fingers.

⚙ Process

  • Stand up and bring the hands beside the body.
  • Raise the hands and bring them parallel to the ground.
  • Wrap your fingers around the thumb and as you exhale spread your fingers as apart as you can.
  • With inhalation turn your palms into fists.
  • Repeat 10 times and relax. Please see Pictures 40 and 41.
