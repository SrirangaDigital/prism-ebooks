Supta Asanas

Lying Down Asanas

According to the Bhagawat Gita, asana means seat on which we sit. Asana also means posture. The main purpose of asana is to be able to sit in one position without discomfort for a long time, so that we can contemplate on a lofty ideal for a long time. The body is compared to a vessel or container and the mind is compared to water in the container. If the vessel is unsteady, water in the vessel, i.e. the mind also becomes unsteady. Even if a breeze passes over the vessel, the water will be disturbed. In the same way, erratic breathing makes the mind unsteady. To have a steady mind we need stable body and smooth even breathing. Practise of asana increases the lung capacity, opens up the chest and strengthens the spinal column and limbs. Therefore asana is a stepping stone to pranayama and higher levels of sadhana.

Asana also means ashraya â€” taking shelter in the postures. Practising asana and pranayama revives our dormant energy. This rejuvenates the tissues, nervous system and regulates blood circulation. According to the laws of physiology, when the body contracts and expands, energy is developed and diseases get cured. This can be achieved through various yoga asanas. With the practise of asana and pranayama, the glands and muscles of the body contract and expand, toxins get expelled and as a result good health is regained or maintained.

The following asanas help in strengthening and energizing the body. They also help in removing gross impurities from the body.

⚈ Anantasana

➲ Meaning

Ananta means infinite.

☞Benefits

This asana stretches and strengthens the buttock, thighs and calf muscles, hamstring, knees, achilles tendons, feet, toes, fingers, wrists, lower arms, elbow, upper arms, shoulder and shoulder blades.

⚙ Process

  • Lie down on your left side with the left elbow on the ground and your headsupported on the left palm.
  • Raise the right leg without bending the knee.
  • When you have raised the right leg to the full extent, bend the right knee and get hold of the right toes with the right hand.
  • Try to straighten the knee and pull the right foot towards the head.
  • Feel the stretch.
  • Remain in this position for 30 seconds and exhale and relax.
  • Repeat the same on the other side.
  • Repeat three times on each side. Slowly increase the duration. Please see Pictures 42 and 43.

⚠ Caution

People with hand, shoulder and hip problems should do this asana with caution.

Bhujangasana, Type 1

➲ Meaning

Bhuja means shoulder. Bhujanga means cobra.

☞Benefits

This asana helps people with asthma and bronchitis, shoulder, back and thyroid problems.

⚙ Process

  • Lie down on your stomach with the chin on the ground.
  • Place the palms next to the shoulders with fingers pointing forward and elbows up. Keep the feet together.
  • With inhalation raise the upper body up to the navel and stretch your neck back as far as you can.
  • Feel the flexing of your spinal column, the stretch on your neck muscles, shoulder muscles and the opening of chest muscles.
  • Remain in this position for as long as you can and be aware of your breathing.
  • With exhalation, return to the starting position and relax. Please refer to Picture 45.
  • Repeat three times.

⚠ Caution

People with acute back and frozen shoulder problems should do this asana with caution.

Ekapada Jatara Parivrittasana

➲ Meaning

Ekapada jatara parivrittasana means twisting of the stomach with one leg.

☞Benefits

This asana strengthens the lower back, buttock, hip, thigh and leg muscles. It reduces fat around the stomach. Keeps the abdominal region healthy, releases tension from the shoulder and neck region, and flexes the hip and spinal column. People with constipation problem will find this asana beneficial.

⚙ Process

  • Lie down on your back with legs straight, big toes together, hands across and elbows straight.
  • With inhalation raise the right leg up by 90 degrees keeping the knee straight.
  • As you exhale bring the right leg towards the left palm without bending the knees. Try to bring the foot on the ground.
  • Turn your neck to the right and look at the right palm. Bring the right ear on the ground.
  • Feel the stretch in the neck, shoulders, upper back, middle back, lower back, hip region and the legs.
  • Remain here for as long as you can with normal breathing.
  • With inhalation raise the leg up and relax.
  • Repeat the same with left leg. Do three times on each leg. Please refer to Pictures 49 and 50.

⚠ Caution

People with back, neck and shoulder injuries should do this asana under the guidance of an experienced teacher.
