Supta Asanas

Lying Down Asanas

According to the Bhagawat Gita, asana means seat on which we sit. Asana also means posture. The main purpose of asana is to be able to sit in one position without discomfort for a long time, so that we can contemplate on a lofty ideal for a long time. The body is compared to a vessel or container and the mind is compared to water in the container. If the vessel is unsteady, water in the vessel, i.e. the mind also becomes unsteady. Even if a breeze passes over the vessel, the water will be disturbed. In the same way, erratic breathing makes the mind unsteady. To have a steady mind we need stable body and smooth even breathing. Practise of asana increases the lung capacity, opens up the chest and strengthens the spinal column and limbs. Therefore asana is a stepping stone to pranayama and higher levels of sadhana.

⚈ Anantasana

➲ Meaning

Ananta means infinite.

☞Benefits

This asana stretches and strengthens the buttock, thighs and calf muscles, hamstring, knees, achilles tendons, feet, toes, fingers, wrists, lower arms, elbow, upper arms, shoulder and shoulder blades.

⚙ Process

  • Lie down on your left side with the left elbow on the ground and your headsupported on the left palm.
  • Raise the right leg without bending the knee.
  • When you have raised the right leg to the full extent, bend the right knee and get hold of the right toes with the right hand.
  • Try to straighten the knee and pull the right foot towards the head.
  • Feel the stretch.
  • Remain in this position for 30 seconds and exhale and relax.
  • Repeat the same on the other side.
  • Repeat three times on each side. Slowly increase the duration. Please see Pictures 42 and 43.

⚠ Caution

People with hand, shoulder and hip problems should do this asana with caution.

Bekasana

➲ Meaning

Beka means tadpole.

☞Benefits

This asana helps people with tired legs, knee and feet related problems by energizing the whole leg.

⚙ Process

  • Lie on your stomach with the chin on the ground.

  • Bend both the knees, catch hold of the feet and press the heels towards the ground next to the waist.

  • Remain in this position for a minute or so. Release the feet and relax.

  • Repeat three more rounds. Please see Picture 44.

⚠ Caution

People with acute knee problem should do this asana with caution.

Bhuja means shoulder. Bhujanga means cobra.

☞Benefits

This asana helps people with asthma and bronchitis, shoulder, back and thyroid problems.

⚙ Process

  • Lie down on your stomach with the chin on the ground.

  • Place the palms next to the shoulders with fingers pointing forward and elbows up. Keep the feet together.

  • With inhalation raise the upper body up to the navel and stretch your neck back as far as you can.

  • Feel the flexing of your spinal column, the stretch on your neck muscles, shoulder muscles and the opening of chest muscles.

  • Remain in this position for as long as you can and be aware of your breathing.

  • With exhalation, return to the starting position and relax. Please refer to Picture 45.

  • Repeat three times.

⚠ Caution

People with acute back and frozen shoulder problems should do this asana with caution.

Bhujangasana, Type 2

➲ Meaning

Bhuja means shoulder. Bhujanga means cobra. This asana is more intense than Bhujangasana Type 1.

☞Benefits

This asana helps people with weak hands, asthma and bronchitis and shoulder, back and thyroid problems.

⚙ Process

  • Lie down on your stomach with the chin on the ground.

  • Place the palms next to the shoulders with fingers pointing toward the feet and elbows up. Keep the feet together.

  • With inhalation raise the upper body upto the navel and stretch your neck as far back as you can.

  • Feel the flexing of your spinal column, the stretch on your neck muscles, shoulder muscles and the opening of chest muscles.

  • Feel the stretch on your wrists and lower arms.

  • Remain in this position for as long as you can.

  • With exhalation, return to the starting position and relax. Please refer to Picture 46.

  • Repeat three times.

⚠ Caution

People with acute back, carpal tunnel and frozen shoulder problems should do this with caution.

Dhanurasana

➲ Meaning

Dhanu means bow.

☞Benefits

This asana energizes the thyroid glands.People with problems related to shoulder, upper back, lower back, breathing and digestion find this asana beneficial.

⚙ Process

  • Lie down on your stomach with the chin on the ground.

  • Place your hands next to the thighs.

  • Bend your knees and catch hold of your feet.

  • With inhalation raise both your head and knees off the ground, as high as you can.

  • Look towards the ceiling. Put a smile on the face.

  • Remain in this position for a few seconds and slowly increase the duration with every repetition.

  • With exhalation come back to the starting position. Please refer to Picture 47.

  • Repeat three times.

⚠ Caution

People with acute back and shoulder problems should do this asana with caution.

➲ Meaning

Dwi pada uttana means both feet up.

☞Benefits

This asana strengthens the core, reduces fat around the abdomen and improves blood circulation in the feet.

⚙ Process

  • Lie down on your back with hands beside the body.

  • With inhalation raise the legs above to 90 degrees without bending the knees.

  • Hold this position for the count of 10 and slowly increase the duration.

  • Slowly exhale and come back to the starting position. Please refer to Picture 48.

  • Repeat three times.

⚠ Caution

People with abdominal problems should do this asana with caution.
