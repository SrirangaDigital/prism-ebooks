Seated asanas

⚈ Adomukha Shwaanasana

➲ Meaning

Adhomukha means facing downward. Shwaana means Dog.

☞Benefits

This asana strengthens the arms and legs, energizes the brain cells, internal organs, spinal column, kidneys and liver, releases tension from the shoulder and back muscles and helps stretch the hamstring muscles, calf muscles and Achilles tendons. It purifies the blood, improves memory, decompresses the internal organs, rehydrates the discs in the spine, slows down the heart rate, massages the heart muscles, and oxygenates the blood.

People with cholesterol problem will find this asana beneficial.

⚙ Process

  • Sit in Vajrasana with big toes touching each other.

  • Bring your shoulders towards the ground by leaning forward and sliding your hands along the ground.

  • The elbows should be straight with the fingers spread wide apart

  • Straighten the knees and push your tailbone up.

  • Bring your heels on the ground.

  • Push the crown of your head towards the ground.

  • Feel the stretch on your shoulders, back, buttocks, hamstring, calf and Achilles tendons.

  • Remain in this position for as long as you can.

  • Come to original position and relax. Please refer to picture 72.

⚠ Caution

People with arm, leg or back injuries should do this asana with caution.

⚈ Aakarna Dhanurasana

➲ Meaning

Karna means ear. Dhanu means bow. Mundakopanishasd says:

Pranavo-dhanuhu-sharo-atmaa
brahma tallakshyam-uchyate
Apramattena-veddhavyam
sharavat-tanmayo-bhavet.

“Om is the bow. The self is the arrow. The Supreme Person is the target. Immersed in this thought one should become like an arrow”. In order for an arrow to hit a target it should have sharpness, strength and speed. We get the speed by pulling the string to the ear. Pulling the string to the ear means, a student should have a sharp hearing and retention ability (sravana and manana). The student should hear it from a teacher and retain it, and only then he or she can hit the target.

☞Benefits

This asana flexes the hips and legs, and releases tension from the feet, calf, thighs, hamstrings, upper back and shoulders.

⚙ Process

  • Sit on your buttocks with legs stretched forward and feet together.

  • Bend your left knee and get hold of the left big toe with the left hand.

  • With inhalation raise your left foot and try to bring it to the left ear.

  • Breathe normally.

  • Remain in this position for as long as you can.

  • Return to the original position and relax.

  • Repeat on the other side. Repeat three times on each side. Please refer to Picture 73 and YouTube: www.youtube.com/watch?v=4HM0zbljt3Q

⚠ Caution

People with back, hip or leg injuries should do this asana with caution.

⚈ Ardha Matsyendrasana

➲ Meaning

Ardha means half. Matsyendra means Lord of fishes. This is one of the 15 asanas mentioned by Swami Swatmarama in Hata Yoga Pradipika.

☞Benefits

This asana strengthens the back muscles, flexes the spinal column and releases stiffness from neck and shoulder muscles. It energizes the pancreas, liver, kidneys, intestines and hip region. It reduces fat around the stomach and waist. People with diabetes, acidity or constipation problems will find this asana beneficial.

⚙ Process

  • Sit on your buttocks.

  • Bend your left knee, and bring the left heel close to the right buttocks with left knee close to the ground.

  • Bend the right knee, and bring the right heel next to the left thigh from outside with right knee facing the ceiling.

  • Raise the left hand and with the left elbow push the right knee further in.

  • Try to bring the left hand next to the right foot and bring the right hand next to left waist from back.

  • As you exhale push your right shoulder back 90 degrees and look over the right shoulder.

  • Feel the twist in your stomach muscles.

  • Feel the stretch around your shoulders, back, neck, spine and legs.

  • Remain in this position for as long as you can.

  • Return to the original position and relax.

  • Repeat on the opposite side.

  • Repeat three times on each side. Please refer to Picture 74.

⚠ Caution

People with acute back and frozen shoulder problems should do this asana with caution.

⚈ Ardha Ustrasana

➲ Meaning

Ardha means half. Ustra means camel.

☞Benefits

This asana strengthens the abdominal muscles and the waist region, while also flexing and strengthening the spinal column. It opens up the lungs, deepens the breathing and activates the thyroid and parathyroid glands.

⚙ Process

  • Sit in Vajrasana.

  • Bring left foot forward with the tibia (shinbone) perpendicular to the ground.

  • Raise the left arm up while keeping the bicep close to the left ear.

  • Bring the right hand on the right ankle.

  • Arch backwards as far as you can and look at the left arm.

  • This should extend the waist region, open the lungs and push the trunk forward.

  • Ensure that you are not leaning to the right side.

  • Remain in this position as long as you can.

  • Return to the original position and relax.

  • Do three times on each side. Please refer to Picture 75.

⚠ Caution

People with knee, back, spinal column or neck injuries should do this asana with caution.

⚈ Badda Konasana

➲ Meaning

Badda means lock. Kona means angle.

☞Benefits

This asana strengthens the uterus and prostate areas. It flexes and strengthens the ankles, stretches the leg muscles and pelvic region, reduces fat around the waist and stomach, and releases toxins from the abdominal muscles and intestines.

⚙ Process

  • Sit on your buttocks.

  • Bend your knees and bring the heels close to the perineum region.

  • Bring the soles of your feet together.

  • Push the knees towards the ground.

  • Feel the stretch on your hamstring, pelvic region, knees and ankles.

  • Lock your fingers around the toes.

  • As you exhale bring your upper body forward.

  • Bring your chest against the feet and forehead towards the ground.

  • Feel the squeeze in the abdominal muscles and the stretch on the back muscles.

  • Remain in this position for as long as you can.

  • As you inhale raise your upper body. Please refer to Picture 76.

  • Repeat three times.

⚠ Caution

People with knee, stomach, hernia or back injuries should do this asana with caution.

⚈ Badda Padmasana, Type 1

➲ Meaning

Badda means locked. Padmasana means Lotus posture.

☞Benefits

This asana helps in strengthening the hands, strengthening and straightening of the spine, opening up the chest muscles and in deepening the breathing. It energizes the internal organs, stomach, reproductive organs, small and large intestines, reduces fat around the waist and releases tension from the shoulders, upper back and middle back regions.

⚙ Process

  • Sit in Padmasana with both heels touching the pelvic region.

  • Cross your hands behind your back and try to get hold of the right foot with left hand and the left foot with right hand.

  • Hold the chin up.

  • Feel the stretch in your shoulders, back, hands and spinal column.

  • Be aware of your breathing.

  • Remain in this position for as long as you are comfortable.

  • Release your hands from the toes and relax. Please refer to Pictures 77a and 77b.

⚠ Caution

People with back, shoulder or knee injuries should do this asana with caution or under supervision.

⚈ Badda Padmasana, Type 2

➲ Meaning

Badda means locked. Padmasana means Lotus posture.

☞Benefits

This is more intense than Badda Padmasana Type 1. This asana energises the internal organs, stomach, small and large intestines and helps in flexing and straightening the spine and reducing the fat around the waist. It strengthens the spine, middle back and hands, and releases tension from the shoulders, upper and middle back.

⚙ Process

  • Sit in Padmasana with heels touching the pelvic region.

  • Cross your arms behind the back and try to get hold of the right foot with the left hand and the left foot with the right hand.

  • Feel the stretch in your shoulders, back, hands and spinal column.

  • With exhalation bend forward and try to bring the forehead to the ground while maintaining the hold on your toes.

  • Remain here for as long as you are comfortable.

  • With inhalation come back to the vertical position.

  • Release your hands from the toes and relax. Please refer to Picture 78.

⚠ Caution

People with back, shoulder or knee injuries should do this asana with caution or under supervision.

⚈ Balasana

➲ Meaning

Bala means child.

☞Benefits

This asana flexes the spinal column, stretches the back muscles and relaxes the mind. People with migraine or headache problems will find this beneficial.

⚙ Process

  • Sit in Vajrasana.

  • As you exhale bring your chest on to your thighs and forehead to the ground.

  • Bring hands next to the feet.

  • The lower lungs and middle lungs are closed and only the upper lungs is active.

  • Remain in this position for as long as you can.

  • Return to the original position and relax.

  • Breathe normally. Please refer to Picture 79.

  • Repeat three times.

⚠ Caution

People with stomach or hernia problems or those with back injury should do this asana with caution.

⚈ Bhadrasana

➲ Meaning

Bhadra means stable, permanent or dependable.

☞Benefits

This asana benefits people with piles. It energizes thesciaticnerves, hamstrings, thigh muscles, knees, calf muscles, ankles, large and small intestines, uterus and prostate. It stretches the abdominal muscles, lower back, middle back, upper back, shoulders and neck muscles and loosens the hip region.

⚙ Process

  • Sit on your buttocks.

  • Bring the heels close to the perineum while keeping the soles of your feet together.

  • Raise your buttocks and sit on your heels.

  • Place hands on your knees and push the knees down towards the ground.

  • Try to straighten the elbows.

  • Keep the back straight and hold the chin up.

  • Feel the stretch in your legs, back, spine, shoulders and neck.

  • Remain in this position for as long as you can. Please refer to Picture 80.

  • Return to the original position and relax.

⚠ Caution

People with ankle, knee, hip or abdominal injuries should do this asana under supervision.

⚈ Bharadwajasana

➲ Meaning

Waja means food. Waja also means mind or spiritual discussion. Bharad means permanent. Bharadwaja means the food which nourishes the body and mind for long time.

☞Benefits

This asana strengthens the back muscles, flexes the spinal column and releases stiffness from neck and shoulder muscles. It energises the pancreas, liver, kidneys, intestines and hip region. It reduces fat around the stomachandwaist.Peoplewithdiabetes, acidity or constipation problems will find this asana beneficial.

⚙ Process

  • This is the simplest of twisting asanas.

  • Sit on your buttocks.

  • Bend both the knees to the right such that the heels are close to right buttock.

  • Bring the right hand behind your back and the left hand on the right heel.

  • Turn your right shoulder back by 90 degrees and look over the right shoulder.

  • Feel the twist in your stomach muscles.

  • Feel the stretch in your shoulders, back, neck, spine and legs.

  • Remain in this position for as long as you can.

  • Return to the original position and relax.

  • Repeat three times on each side. Please refer to Picture 81.

⚠ Caution

People with knee, back, spinal column or neck injury should do this asana with caution.

⚈ Ekapada Adhomukha Shwanasana

➲ Meaning

Ekapada Adhomukha Shwanasana means one legged downward dog posture.

☞Benefits

This asana increases the heart’s volume, flow of blood to the brain and the memory power. It energizes the kidneys and internal organs and strengthens the hands, shoulders, back, hip region, thigh muscles, calf muscles, ankles and Achilles tendons.

⚙ Process

  • Sit in Vajrasana.

  • Eka pada Ado mukha Swanasana Step 1

  • With inhalation raise your hands up ith the elbows straight. Bring your palms together and look up.

  • With exhalation move into Shashankasana.

  • Now lifting your tailbone high, straighten the knees and try to bring the heels fully on the ground.

  • Keeping the elbows straight move into Adhomukha Shwaanasana.

  • With inhalation, raise the right leg up and move into Ekapada Adhomukha Shwaanasana. In this posture the hands, back and right leg should be in one straight line.

  • Try to bring the crown of your head on the ground while keeping the elbows straight.

  • Remain here for as long as you can.

  • With exhalation bring the leg back to the original position.

  • With inhalation repeat with the other leg.

  • Repeat three times on each legs.

  • With exhalation come back to Shashankasana.

  • With inhalation raise your hands up with the elbows straight and palms together.

  • With exhalation relax in Vajrasana. Please refer to Picture 82-85.

⚠ Caution

People with injured back, feet, hands or shoulders should do this asana under an experienced teacher.

⚈ Ekapada Prasaranasana

➲ Meaning

Ekapada Prasaranasana means one legged extension.

☞Benefits

This asana strengthens the hands, shoulders, back, hip region, leg muscles, abdominal muscles and toes.

⚙ Process

  • Sit in Vajrasana.

  • With inhalation raise your hands up.

  • Keep your elbows straight, bring your palms together and look up.

  • With exhalation come to Shashankasana.

  • Keep your elbows straight with palms on the ground and fingers apart.

  • Forehead should be on the ground and buttocks should be on the heels.

  • Straighten the knees while supporting the body on tips of the toes and palms of hands. Keeping elbows straight, push the shoulders ahead of palms and come to Dwi pada Prasaranasana

  • Body weight should be distributed on the tip of the toes and palms.

  • With inhalation, raise the left leg up, to move into

  • Ekapada Prasaranasana.

  • Remain here for as long as you can.

  • With exhalation bring the leg back to Dwipada Prasaranasana.

  • With inhalation repeat with other leg.

  • Repeat three times on each leg.

  • Return to Shashankasana on exhalation.

  • With inhalation raise your hands up with the elbows straight and palms together.

  • With exhalation relax in Vajrasana. Please refer to Pictures 86 and 87.

⚠ Caution

People with injured back, feet, hands or shoulders should do this asana under the supervision of an experienced teacher.

⚈ Gomukhasana

➲ Meaning

Go means cow. Go also means Vedas (knowledge). Mukha means face, a serene face. The face becomes serene like one who has studied and understood the Vedas.

☞Benefits

This asana releases tension from the shoulders and upper arms and flexes the shoulder muscles. People with frozen shoulders, hunch back or breathing disorders will find this beneficial.

⚙ Process

  • Sit down with legs straight.

  • Bend the left knee and bring the left heels next to the right buttock.

  • Bend the right knee and bring the right knee over the left knee and the right heel next to left buttock.

  • Bend your left elbow with elbow pointing down.

  • Bring your left lower arm behind your spinal column with palm facing outwards.

  • Raise your right hand up.

  • Bend your right elbow with elbow facing the ceiling.

  • Bring the right palm over the left. Try to lock your fingers.

  • Bring the right upper arm behind the head and try to push your upper arm with head.

  • Chin up and try to look at the ceiling.

  • Feel the stretch in the shoulder and arm region.

  • Be aware of the breathing.

  • Remain in this position for as long as you can.

  • Return to the original position and relax.

  • Alternate the legs and hands.

  • Repeat three times. Please refer to Pictures 88a and 88b.

⚠ Caution

People with neck or shoulder injuries should do this asana with caution.

⚈ Janu Shirasasana, Type 1

➲ Meaning

Janu means knee. Janu Shirasasana means bringing the forehead to the knee.

☞Benefits

This asana energizes the calf, hamstring and thigh muscles, flexes and elongates the spinal column, squeezes the stomach muscles, stretches the back muscles and releases tension from shoulders and neck.

⚙ Process

  • Stretch the left leg forward and bend your right knee.

  • Bring the right heel towards the perineum.

  • With inhalation raise both your hands up keeping the elbows straight.

  • Bring the palms together and look at the palms.

  • As you exhale, lean forward stretching and elongating your back to bring the palms around the left foot.

  • Bring the chest towards the thigh and forehead towards the knee.

  • Feel the stretch in your back muscles and squeeze on your stomach muscles.

  • Remain in this position for as long as you can.

  • With inhalation raise your body and hands up and relax with your hands beside the body.

  • Do the same sequence with the other leg.

  • Repeat three times. Please refer to Picture 89.

⚠ Caution

People with injured back, knees or shoulders should do this with caution.
