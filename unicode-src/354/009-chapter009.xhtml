⚈ Ardha Chandrasana, Type 1

➲ Meaning

Ardha means half. Chandra means moon. This is a half moon posture.

☞Benefits

This asana strengthens the leg muscles, releases tension from the neck and shoulder muscles and improves the body balance.

⚙ Process

  • Spread your feet as apart as you can.
  • Bend your right knee and bring your right thigh parallel to the ground.
  • Bring your right hand next to right foot and keep the right hand elbow straight.
  • Keep the left knee straight with the left foot completely on the ground.
  • Bring the left hand on the left leg.
  • Push your left shoulder back and look through your shoulder.
  • Remain here for as long as you can.
  • Be aware of your breathing.
  • Inhale and straighten the knees and relax.
  • Repeat on the other side. Please refer to Picture 122.
  • Repeat 3 times on each side.

⚠ Caution

People with knee, leg, neck or shoulder problems should do this asana with caution.

⚈ Ardha Chandrasana, Type 2

➲ Meaning

⚈ Ardha Kati Chakrasana, Type 1

➲ Meaning

Ardha means half. Kati means waist. In this asana the side muscles become like an arc.

☞Benefits

This asana energizes the rib cage and side, hip, shoulders and neck muscles. It strengthens the leg and hand muscles and helps in aligning the spinal column.

⚙ Process

  • Stand upright with big toes together.
  • Bring your body, neck and head in a straight line.
  • Inhale and raise your right hand up and bring the right biceps next to the right ear.
  • As you exhale lean on left side as much as you can while keeping the contact between biceps and ear.
  • Slide your left hand down.
  • Look at the right hand.
  • Feel the stretch on your right side.
  • Remain in this position for as long as you can.
  • Inhale and raise your hand and exhale and bring right hand next to the body and relax.
  • Repeat on the other side.
  • Repeat this 3 times on each side. Please refer to Picture 124.

⚠ Caution

People with hip or shoulder injury should do this asana with caution.

⚈ Chakrasana, Type 1

➲ Meaning

Chakra means wheel. Chakrasana Type 1 is a preparation for doing Chakrasana Type 2.

☞Benefits

This asana flexes the back muscles and energizes the spinal column. It releases tension from shoulder muscles, opens up the chest muscles and deepens the breathing. It strengthens the hands, leg and stomach muscles and activates the thyroid, parathyroid, thymus and adrenal glands.

⚙ Process

  • Lie down on your back.
  • Bend your knees with the feet apart.
  • Bend your hands with the elbows pointing up.
  • Place your hands next to the head with the fingers point towards the feet.
  • Inhale and raise the body up so that the elbows are straight, waist is up, feet
  • are flat on the ground and the head and neck are stretched out.
  • Both hands and feet should be shoulder width apart.
  • Be aware of the breathing.
  • Feel the stretch.
  • Remain in this position for as long as you can.
  • With exhale, gently bring the body back on the ground and relax.
  • Repeat 3 times. Please refer to Pictures 126 and 127.

⚠ Caution

People with back, shoulder problems and spinal issues should do this asana with caution.

⚈ Ekapada Hastasana, Type 1

➲ Meaning

Ekapada Hastasana means bringing a single leg and hand together.

☞Benefits

This asana loosens the hip region, strengthens and energizes the toes, feet, calves, thighs, buttocks and the leg muscles. It activates both the left and right brain hemispheres and both the emotional and intellectual personality. It improves concentration.

⚙ Process

  • Stand upright and bring big toes together.
  • Bring back, neck and head in one line.
  • Bend your right knee and lock your knees.
  • Pull the thigh towards the chest and bring calf muscles and thigh together.
  • Feel the flexing in the hip region and the stretch in your buttock muscles.
  • Be aware of the breathing.
  • Remain in this position for as long as you comfortably can.
  • Bring the leg down and relax.
  • Repeat with the other leg.
  • Repeat 3 times on each leg. Please refer to Picture 129.

⚠ Caution

People with vertigo, body trembling or mobility problems should do this asana with caution or under supervision.

⚈ Garudasana

➲ Meaning

Garuda means Eagle, the vehicle of Lord Vishnu.

☞Benefits

This asana strengthens the leg muscles and ankles and energizes the prostate and uterus. It increases the mental balance.

⚙ Process

  • Stand up with big toes touching each other.
  • Raise the right leg up and wrap the right leg around the left leg.
  • Bend the left elbow and bring the left hand in front of you.
  • Wrap the right hand around your left hand and bring palms together.
  • Look ahead through your palms.
  • Chin up.
  • With exhale bend your left knee and try to bring the left thigh parallel to the ground.
  • Be aware of your breathing.
  • Remain here for as long as you can.
  • Inhale and come up and relax.
  • Repeat the same with alternate legs and hands for 3 times. Please refer to Picture 131.

⚠ Caution

People with knee problems, hernia or abdominal problems should do this asana with caution.

⚈ Natarajasana

➲ Meaning

Nataraja means the king of dance. Shiva is also known as Nataraja because he performs the tandava nritya (Shiva’s dance) at the time of pralaya (universal dissolution).

☞Benefits

This asana strengthens the legs, increases balance and releases tension from the shoulders. It flexes the spinal column and waist and strengthens the muscles in the feet.

⚙ Process

  • Stand up with big toes touching each other.
  • Stand straight with the back, neck and head in one line.
  • With inhalation bring the right leg back, bend the right knee and get hold of the right foot with the right hand.
  • Pull the right leg up and try to straighten the right knee still holding right foot with right hand.
  • Try to raise the foot above your head level.
  • Raise your left hand in front of you.
  • Look at your left palm.
  • Feel the flexing of the back and hip region.
  • Be aware of your breathing.
  • Remain here for as long as you can.
  • Exhale and bring your leg down and relax.
  • Repeat on the alternate side. Please refer to Picture 132.

⚠ Caution

People with back, knee or leg problems should do this asana under supervision.

⚈ Pada Hastasana

➲ Meaning

Pada means feet. Hasta means hands. Bringing the hands next to the feet is Pada Hastasana.

☞Benefits

This asana strengthens the calf muscles, hamstrings, buttocks and hip region and flexes the spinal column. It releases tension from the back, shoulders and neck muscles and reduces fat around the stomach.

⚙ Process

  • Stand upright with big toes touching each other.
  • Body weight is equally distributed on the feet.
  • With inhalation raise your hands up and look at the palms.
  • As you exhale, bend forward and try to bring your forehead towards the knees.
  • Bring the hands next to the feet on the ground.
  • Keep the knees straight. Remain here for as long as you can.
  • With inhalation raise your head and hands up.
  • With exhalation bring the hands down and relax.
  • Repeat 3 times. With every repetition try to bring the forehead closer to the knee and the hands further down towards the ground. Please refer to Picture 133.

⚠ Caution

People with hip, back or leg injuries should do this asana with caution.
