Surya Namaskara

Surya Namaskara is for the body, mind and soul. Every morning at sunrise, doing Surya Namaskara facing the East, bestows good health and long life. Also, Prana Shakti increases energy level and stamina. Eye sight and glow in the face improves and one becomes radiant and effulgent like a sun. It is also believed that there will not be any dearth in life. Doing Surya Namaskara regularly increases oxygen content in the blood, improves body immunity and strengthens the respiratory, digestive, circulatory, nervous, reproductive and excretory systems.

Surya Namaskara is beneficial for people with blood pressure, diabetes, obesity and depression problems. Above all, it helps in invoking the Grace of the Lord in the Sun, who is also residing in our heart.

The Sun enlivens the whole world. At dawn, birds start singing with joy seeing the sunrise. As the sun sets, all birds go to their nests. We also feel energetic as the sun rises and we feel drained of energy as the sun sets. There is a link between the sun and the energy level in the body. By doing Surya Namaskara, we are linking the Prana Shakti (energy level) in our body to the Prana Shakti in the Sun. This linking process is Surya Namaskara. There is a Prana in the sun, and within the Prana resides the Supreme Lord. In our body also, there is a Prana and within this Prana resides the Supreme

Lord. The Rig Veda mantra describes the benefits of praying to the Lord in the sun.

In summary, the Vedic mantras say: ‘Give us a healthy body with long life so that we can continue our sadhana — practise for the whole life. Let there be no deformity, accidents or untimely death for us which curtails our sadhana. May we get a permanent body, i.e. liberation, so that there is no pain of birth and death.’

Some of the Surya Namaskara mantras within the Vedas and Upanishads are listed here. Think of the meanings of these mantras while doing the Surya Namaskara for maximizing the benefits.

Surya Namaskara Mantra in Katopanishad / Mundakopanishad / Shwetaswatara Upanishad

Na Tatra Suryo Bhati
na Chandra Tarakam nema Vidyuto bhanti
kutoyam Agnihi
Tameva bhantam
anubhati sarvam
Tasya bhasa
sarvam idam vibhati

The state of realization, moksha, described in this mantra is, ‘where the Sun does not shine, nor the Moon nor the Stars, nor Lightening shines. How then can this earthly fire illumine the Self? Verily everything shines by Him reflecting His glory. This whole world is illumined with His Light’.

Surya Namaskara Mantra in Mundakopanishad

Hiranmaye pare koshe
virajam brahma nishkalam
tat shubhram jyotisham jyotis
tad yad atma vido viduhu

‘The stainless, indivisible Brahman, the pure, the light of all lights, is in the innermost sheath of the golden hue, the highest. This is what the knowers of the Self know.’

Surya Namaskara Mantra in Gayatri Dhyana Sloka

Prodyad Aditya varnascha
surya mandala madyagah
chakra shanka dharonkastha
dordwayo dhyeya eva cha
Dhyeyah sada
savitrmandalamadhyavarthee
Narayanaha sarasijasanasannivihtah
keyuravan makarakundalavan kireetee
haaree hiranmayavapuh
dhritashankhachakrah

‘When you sit for meditation, think of the rising, orange coloured Sun in your heart centre. Within the Sun, think of the Lord seated in Padmasana with two hands on the knees, and holding discus and conch in the other two hands, wearing crocodile shaped ear rings and a crown. Thinking of the Lord this way, repeat the Gayatri mantra.’

Surya Namaskara Mantra in Rig Veda

Tacchakshudeva hitam shukramacharat
pashyema sharadah shatam jivema
sharadah shatam nandama sharadah
shatam modama sharadah shatam
bhavama sharadah shatam shrunavama
sharadah shatam prabruvama sharadah
shatam ajitaah syama shradah shatam
jyok pashyema Surya drishe Om.

‘May we see and do actions pleasing to God for 100 years. That Sun, who does good, is like an eye to the God, the pure light that rises in the east. Let us mix in joy for 100 years with our own people. May we be happy for 100 years. May we be bright with good fame for 100 years. May we hear pleasant things for 100 years. May we speak good things for 100 years. May we not be soiled by bad defeat for 100 years. May we not be a burden to others when we are old and infirm. May we see God in all for 100 years.’

Surya Namaskara Mantra in Aitareya Upanishad

Om udvayam tamasaspari
jyotish pashyanta uttaram
Devam devatraa
Suryamagana jyothiruthamam

‘I am awakened now from my ignorance, seeing the most auspicious Light. This Light is being worshipped by all divine beings and I am merging myself in that Light.’

Surya Namaskara Mantra in Isavasya Upanishad

Om Hirnmayena PatreNa
Satyasya apihitam Mukham
Tat Twam PooshannapaavruNu
Satyadharmaya Drishtaye

‘O Protector Sun, please remove the golden plate covering your face (by whose radiance, I am unable to see the Truth) so that the Truth may be revealed to me.’

Surya Namaskara Mantra in Bhagawat Gita, Chapter 15

Yadadityagatam tejo
jagat bhasayate akhilam
Yacchandramasi yacchagnau
tat tejo viddhi mamakam

Lord Shri Krishna says — ‘The splendour of the sun, which dissipates the darkness of this whole world, comes from Me. The splendour of the moon and the splendour of fire are also from Me.’

Surya Namaskara Mantra in Bhagawat Gita, Chapter 13

jyotisham api tat jyotihi
tamasah paramuchyate
jnanam jneyam jnānagamyam
hridi sarvasya dhistitam

‘He is the source of light in all luminous objects. He is beyond the darkness of matter and is unmanifested. He is knowledge, He is the object of knowledge, and He is the goal of knowledge. He is situated in everyone’s heart.’

Surya Namaskara given here has 16 steps. This has numerological significance. Bhagawat Gita has 18 chapters. The number 18 is divine, the highest. The number 18 means the Lord (Purushottama). The number 17 means akshara (Lakshmi). The number 16 is the individual soul. This individual soul is bound and caught up by 15 things. By His Grace if this 16 (individual soul) turns its attention to 18 (the Lord), 17 (Akshara — Lakshmi) paves the way to the souls journey towards Him. If not, 17 (Akshara — Lakshmi) binds 16 (individual soul) with 15 aspects. This is the message of the 15th Chapter of Bhagawat Gita and this is the message of whole Bhagawat Gita. The 15 things which binds the individual soul are:

  1. Kham: Space
  2. Vayu: Air
  3. Agni: Fire
  4. Aapa: Water
  5. Pritvi: Earth
  6. Karmedriya and Jnanedriya: Legs, hands, mouth, anus, genital, eyes, ears, nose, tongue, skin
  7. Mana: Mind
  8. Ahara: Food – type of food we eat
  9. Veerya: Stamina – getting fat or lean
  10. Tapa: Thinking
  11. Mantra: Talking
  12. Karma: Action — what we think, that we do.
  13. Loka: Our possessions, properties
  14. Nama: Name and Fame — we do things for name and fame
  15. Shradda: Belief – family traditions, social beliefs, our convictions etc.
  16. The 16 step Surya Namaskara is the soul’s prayer — may this soul (16) turn its attention to the Lord (18) and may Lakshmi
  17. remove all the hurdles in our path.
