Mudras and Bandha

Mudra means symbol. Mudra means insignia. Mudra means commitment. Samudra means full commitment. That is why God is called as Samudra, as He has full commitment in everything and never does anything half-heartedly.

According to Gheranda Samhita, Mudras and Bandhas are done to steady the body. Mudras and Bandhas help in preventing the dissipation of energy, and help in redirecting the energy. Bandhas are the lock and mudras are the lock over a lock.

Ha means Sun, Ta means Moon. The seat of moon centre is the Sahasrara chakra, and the seat of Sun system is the manipura chakra at the navel. Our active life continues as long as the nectar remains in the sahasrara chakra at the crown. Just like pressing the accelerator in the car draws more fuel, when we breathe rapidly, we draw more nectar from the sahasrara chakra which flows into the manipura chakra where it gets consumed. Bandhas like Jalandhara bandha, Kriyas like Vasudeva Kriya, Viparita Karani, Asanas such as Shirasasana, Sarvangasana, Vrischikasana etc. reduce the consumption of the nectar (amruta) at manipura chakra and preserve the body’s vitality.

Through manipulating our Prana or vital energy, we can direct it towards better health, well being and personal transformation.

Mudra is defined as Mudham Dadaati Devaanam Dravayasya Asuraarapi – ‘That by which the divine beings get happiness and the negative forces and diseases are driven away’.’ Mudras are used in Tantra puja.

Mudras are classified into:

  • Kaya Mudra (mudra through body —Viparitakarani, Yoga mudra, Manduki Mudra etc)

  • Mana Mudra (mudra through the senses on the face, that is using lips, eyes, nose, ear, tongue – Shambhavi, Kaki, Akashi, Nasika Dristi, Shanmukhi, Khechari)

  • Bandha (Jaalandhar, Uddiyan, Moola and Maha)

  • Adhara Mudra (supporting mudras-Aswini, Vrajoli etc)

  • Hasta Mudra (mudras through hands- balance the

    • Mahabhootas like fire, water, air, earth and ether elements and

    • Tanmaatras like taste, touch, smell and sound in the body and

    • Doshas in the body like kapha (phlegm), pitta (fire) and vata (air).
