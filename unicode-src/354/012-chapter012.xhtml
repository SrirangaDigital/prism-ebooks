Mudras and Bandha

Mudra means symbol. Mudra means insignia. Mudra means commitment. Samudra means full commitment. That is why God is called as Samudra, as He has full commitment in everything and never does anything half-heartedly.

According to Gheranda Samhita, Mudras and Bandhas are done to steady the body. Mudras and Bandhas help in preventing the dissipation of energy, and help in redirecting the energy. Bandhas are the lock and mudras are the lock over a lock.

Ha means Sun, Ta means Moon. The seat of moon centre is the Sahasrara chakra, and the seat of Sun system is the manipura chakra at the navel. Our active life continues as long as the nectar remains in the sahasrara chakra at the crown. Just like pressing the accelerator in the car draws more fuel, when we breathe rapidly, we draw more nectar from the sahasrara chakra which flows into the manipura chakra where it gets consumed. Bandhas like Jalandhara bandha, Kriyas like Vasudeva Kriya, Viparita Karani, Asanas such as Shirasasana, Sarvangasana, Vrischikasana etc. reduce the consumption of the nectar (amruta) at manipura chakra and preserve the body’s vitality.

Through manipulating our Prana or vital energy, we can direct it towards better health, well being and personal transformation.

Mudra is defined as Mudham Dadaati Devaanam Dravayasya Asuraarapi – ‘That by which the divine beings get happiness and the negative forces and diseases are driven away’.’ Mudras are used in Tantra puja.

Mudras are classified into:

  • Kaya Mudra (mudra through body —Viparitakarani, Yoga mudra, Manduki Mudra etc)
  • Mana Mudra (mudra through the senses on the face, that is using lips, eyes, nose, ear, tongue – Shambhavi, Kaki, Akashi, Nasika Dristi, Shanmukhi, Khechari)
  • Bandha (Jaalandhar, Uddiyan, Moola and Maha)
  • Adhara Mudra (supporting mudras-Aswini, Vrajoli etc)
  • Hasta Mudra (mudras through hands- balance the
    • Mahabhootas like fire, water, air, earth and ether elements and
    • Tanmaatras like taste, touch, smell and sound in the body and
    • Doshas in the body like kapha (phlegm), pitta (fire) and vata (air).

Hasta Mudra (Hand mudra)

The body is made up of 5 elements – Earth, Water, Fire, Air and Ether. When these five elements are not in balance we get various physical and psychological ailments. The energy from the body radiates out mainly through the tip of the nose, lips, fingertips and toes. When the thumb is brought in contact with the other fingers, energy is directed back to vitalize various parts of our body. This is called a circuit bypass. Mudras and Bhandas go hand in hand. Bandhas help in locking in the energy, whereas mudras help in redirecting the energy.

Mudras are the expression of our inner feeling. In the Tantra shastra, Mudra is defined as ‘the one which gives happiness’. Mudras form a part of yoga and are together referred to as Yoga Mudra. Mudras done using hands are called Hasta Mudra. Indian classical dances such as Bharata Natyam, Yakshagana, Mohini Aattam, Kathakali, Kuchupudi, Oddissi make use of mudras to express their artforms. In the temples deities show different mudras. Of those the most well-known mudra is the abhaya mudra (reassuring mudra). In Tirupathi, Balaji’s statue depicts that ‘if you surrender to my feet, your problems are knee deep, and you can easily walk through them.’

The five fingers represent the five elements (tatvas):

  • Thumb: Fire (agni)
  • Index Finger: Air (vayu)
  • Middle finger: Space (akasha)
  • Ring Finger: Earth (prithvi)
  • Small finger: Water (jala)

Fire is the most important of the five elements. When the fire element becomes weak in the body, various ailments come to the body. Through the practise of mudras the fire element (thumb) is joined with the other elements (other fingers) to vitalize those elements also.

☞Benefits

This helps in curing abdominal problems. It reduces the fat around the waist and stomach, flexes the back muscles and energizes the lungs.

⚙ Process

  • Sit in Padmasana and bring the heels as in as you can.
  • Keep the back, neck and head in one line.
  • Place the palms on the feet.
  • Exhale slowly and bend forward and touch the ground with the forehead.
  • Remain here as long as you can.
  • If you retain the pose for a long time, you can breathe as usual.
  • Inhale and come up.
  • Instead of keeping the hands on the heels, you can take them to the back and catch hold of the left wrist with your right hand.
  • Please refer to Picture 211. Repeat a few times.

Moola Bandha

➲ Meaning

Moola means root.

☞Benefits

Moola Bandha enables one in doing sadhana. It gives Dhatu-Pushti (nerve-vigour) and relieves constipation. This helps in awakening the kundalini Shakti and benefits people with piles related problems. Moola Bandha can be combined with the practise of concentration, meditation, pranayama and all other Yogic Kriyas.

⚙ Process

  • Sit in Siddasana.
  • Bring the left heel near the anus and right heel above it.
  • Keep your body, head and neck in one line. Hold the chin up.
  • Place the palms on the knees.
  • Exhale and squeeze and pull the anus muscles up so that the downward flowing Apana Prana is pushed upwards.
  • The Apana unites with the upward flowing energy — Prana, to create the pranaapana vayu which then enters the Sushumna Nadi.
  • Hold this position for as long as you can. Relax and repeat the same and do as long as you can.

Jaalandhara Bandha

➲ Meaning

Jaala means net or mesh.

☞Benefits

In this Bandha, you are spreading the net in the throat to catch the nectar trickling down from the Moon centre in the head so that it will not fall into the Sun centre (manipura chakra) and get consumed in the digestive fire (jataragni). This Bandha slows down the aging process and activates the vishuddi chakra.

⚙ Process

  • Sit in Siddasana, Padmasana, Ardha Padmasana or Vajrasana.
  • If you cannot sit on the floor you can sit on a chair.
  • Keep your body, head and neck in one line.
  • Hold the chin up.
  • Place your hands on your knees.
  • Exhale and bring the chin and collar bone together.
  • Remain in this position for as long as you can. Please refer to Picture 212.
  • Inhale, bring your chin back to the normal position and relax.

Uddayana Bandha

➲ Meaning

Ut means up.

☞Benefits

This Bandha makes the Prana to fly up through the sushumna nadi. It activates the digestive fire, activates the manipura chakra, reduces the fat around the stomach. Keeps stomach, intestine, uterus and prostate healthy. This Bandha pushes the energy from swadistana and manipura chakra to the higher chakras.

⚙ Process

  • Uddayana Bandha can be done by sitting or standing.
  • Those who can do this Bandha can do the nauli kriya well.
  • Sit in Siddasana, Padmasana, Ardha Padmasana or Vajrasana.
  • Keep your body, head and neck in one line.
  • Hold the chin up.
  • If you are standing keep the legs slightly apart.
  • Bring your palms on your knees.
  • Keep the elbows straight and lean forward.
  • Exhale completely and pull the diaphragm (the muscular portion between the thoracic cavity and stomach) up.
  • This will draw the abdominal muscles back.
  • Hold it for as long as you can and exhale and relax. Please refer to Picture 213.
  • Repeat few times.

Maha Bandha

➲ Meaning

Maha means great.

☞Benefits

Maha Bandha has the benefit of all 3 Bandhas – moola, uddiyana and jaalandara bandha. This Bandha helps in curing the diseases and increases the lifespan.

⚙ Process

  • Maha Bandha is the combination of all 3 bandhas – moola, uddiyana and jaalandara bandha all done at a time.
  • Sit in Siddasana, Padmasana, Ardha Padmasana or Vajrasana.
  • Keep your body, head and neck in one line.
  • Hold the chin up.
  • Place your palms on your knees.
  • Exhale, pull the anus and diaphragm up and pull the abdominal muscles back.
  • Bring the chin and collar bone together.
  • Remain here for as long as you can. Please refer to Picture 214.
  • Inhale and relax.

Maha Vedha

➲ Meaning

Maha means great. Vedha means piercing.

☞Benefits

This helps in curing haemorrhoids, piles, enlargement of spleen, indigestion, gulma (chronic gastritis), leprosy, constipation, fever and other diseases. Life span is increased. It confers great siddhis on the practitioners.

⚙ Process

  • Sit in Siddasana, Padmasana or Ardha Padmasana.
  • Keep your body, head and neck in one line. Keep the chin up.
  • Bring your palms on your knees.
  • Draw the breath slowly.
  • Retain the breath.
  • Press the chin against the chest and pull the anus and diaphragm up and pull the abdominal muscles back.
  • Place the palms on the ground.
  • Rest the body on the palms.
  • Raise the buttocks slowly and bump them gently against the ground.
  • The asana must be intact and firm when you raise the buttocks.
  • Bring the chin and collar bone together.
  • Remain here for as long as you can.
  • Inhale and relax.
  • There is not much difference between Maha mudra, Maha bandha and Maha vedha.
  • Please refer to Picture 215.
